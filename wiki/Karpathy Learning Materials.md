# Karpathy Learning Materials

## Summary

Karpathy's teaching materials are centered on code-first understanding: build the thing from scratch, inspect the moving parts, and use small implementations to make modern AI systems less mysterious.

## Main Tracks

### Neural Networks: Zero to Hero

Zero to Hero teaches neural networks from scratch in code. It starts with backpropagation and progresses through language modeling, MLPs, BatchNorm, WaveNet-style modeling, GPT, and tokenization.

Best use:

- Learn neural network fundamentals by implementing them.
- Build intuition for tensors, gradients, training loops, and language models.
- Prepare for reading or modifying small GPT-style repositories.

### CS231n

Karpathy designed and was primary instructor for Stanford's first deep learning class, CS231n: Convolutional Neural Networks for Visual Recognition. This is the older academic course track around computer vision and deep learning foundations.

### LLM101n

LLM101n is [[Eureka Labs]]' first product. It is described by Eureka Labs as an undergraduate-level AI course where students train their own AI.

### General-Audience LLM Videos

Karpathy's site links to general-audience videos on LLM fundamentals and practical use of LLMs. These are useful before, after, or alongside the technical track depending on the learner's depth.

## Suggested Learning Sequence

Inference:

1. Watch a general-audience LLM overview if the learner is new to LLMs.
2. Work through Zero to Hero through the GPT and tokenizer lessons.
3. Read or run [[Karpathy Projects]] such as micrograd, makemore, and nanoGPT.
4. Use LLM101n when the course materials are available and the goal is a more complete curriculum.
