# SOP - Item 113: File Quarterly Form 941

## Purpose

Prepare and file quarterly IRS Form 941 for payroll wages and withholding.

## When To Use

Use this for each payroll quarter: Q1, Q2, Q3, and Q4.

## Owner

- Role: Accounting / Wes
- Backup: Tax professional or approved accounting support

## Category

Accounting / Payroll Tax

## Inputs Needed

- Payroll records for the quarter
- Number of employees paid during the quarter
- Gross wages
- Federal income tax withheld
- Social Security and Medicare wage totals
- Monthly tax liability breakdown
- EFTPS access through Login.gov, if payment is due

## Tools / Systems

- IRS Form 941
- IRS Free File Fillable Forms or downloaded PDF
- EFTPS
- Login.gov authentication for EFTPS
- Payroll records

## Steps

1. Open the current IRS Form 941 from the IRS website.
2. Confirm the correct form year and quarter.
3. Check only one quarter box: Q1, Q2, Q3, or Q4.
4. Enter the business name, EIN, and address.
5. On line 1, enter the number of employees who received wages during the quarter.
6. On line 2, enter total wages paid during the quarter.
7. On line 3, enter federal income tax withheld.
8. On lines 5a through 5d, enter Social Security and Medicare wage information as applicable.
9. Confirm total taxes on line 6.
10. Confirm line 10 after adjustments, usually the same as line 6 if there are no adjustments.
11. In Part 2, select the correct deposit schedule. For most small businesses in the source, use monthly depositor unless records say otherwise.
12. Enter the monthly tax liability breakdown.
13. Leave Part 3 blank unless a valid adjustment applies.
14. Leave the third-party designee section blank unless intentionally authorizing someone.
15. Sign and date the form with the correct title.
16. If payment is owed, go to `https://www.eftps.gov`, choose the EFTPS payment/login path, and authenticate using Login.gov.
17. Save proof of filing and payment.
18. Confirm quarterly totals will reconcile to year-end W-2 totals.

## Decision Rules

- File the correct 941 for each quarter.
- Use Form 941-X to correct prior-quarter mistakes instead of fixing them in a later quarter.
- W-2 wages and all four 941 filings should reconcile at year end.
- Confirm deposit schedule with tax records or a tax professional if uncertain.

## Common Mistakes

- Forgetting one of the four quarterly filings.
- Reporting the wrong number of employees.
- Entering totals that do not match payroll records.
- Trying to correct earlier quarters on the wrong quarter's form.
