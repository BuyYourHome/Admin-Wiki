# BYH Single-Member Structure Review

## Question

After the Buy Your Home, LLC membership interest was assigned to the Browning Family Revocable Trust, BYH appears to have moved from a multi-member LLC to a single-member LLC. Wes asked whether giving Wes and Jenny a 1% ownership interest is a viable option to reduce single-member vulnerability.

## Current Document Baseline

The copied estate documents show that Wes and Jeanette/Jenny assigned their combined 100% membership interest in Buy Your Home, LLC to the Browning Family Revocable Trust dated June 21, 2023.

That means the Trust is the current member/owner under the assignment. Voting and member-consent rights should be exercised by the then-serving trustee(s) of the Trust, unless BYH's operating agreement or later records say otherwise.

## 1% Ownership Option

Giving Wes and Jenny a 1% ownership interest could make BYH a multi-member LLC again, but this should not be treated as a simple paperwork fix.

Issues to review with attorney and CPA:

- Whether the 1% interests would be real economic interests with genuine rights, duties, tax allocations, and capital accounts.
- Whether the transfer would partially undo the estate-planning purpose of moving BYH into the Trust.
- Whether the 1% interests would create probate exposure or require separate transfer-on-death, trust assignment, or will treatment.
- Whether the transfer has gift, income-tax, basis, accounting, or partnership-tax consequences.
- Whether the arrangement would be respected for asset-protection purposes if the only purpose is to avoid single-member treatment.
- Whether Wes and Jenny holding interests personally creates creditor exposure that the Trust assignment was intended to reduce.

The option may be viable if there is a real business and estate-planning reason for the second member interests, but it should be attorney-designed rather than implemented as nominal ownership only.

## Other Structure Options To Review

1. Keep BYH fully trust-owned and strengthen the operating agreement.
   - Name the Browning Family Revocable Trust as sole member.
   - Define trustee voting/member-consent authority.
   - Define manager and signing authority separately from member ownership.
   - Add incapacity, death, successor trustee, and records-update procedures.

2. Keep BYH as a parent entity and move lawsuit-prone assets into subsidiaries.
   - Heritage Management LLC can hold long-term property if ownership and property records confirm the chain.
   - Property-specific LLCs may be considered for higher-risk assets.

3. Use a real second member only if there is a substantive reason.
   - Possible second members could include an individual, another trust, or another entity.
   - The second member should have documented rights and economics.
   - The second member's succession path must be documented.

4. Divide the revocable trust structure into two separate trusts.
   - One trust could be created for Wes's beneficial interest and one trust for Jenny's beneficial interest.
   - BYH could then be owned by the two trusts as members, rather than by one joint revocable trust.
   - The trust dispositive provisions could be designed to stay substantially similar, but each trust would need its own grantor, trustee, successor trustee, beneficiary, tax, amendment, revocation, and funding provisions.
   - BYH's operating agreement would need to show both trusts as members and define voting, manager appointment, deadlock handling, transfer restrictions, death/incapacity handling, and successor trustee recognition.
   - This may preserve trust-based estate planning while creating a genuine multi-member LLC structure, if the trusts are respected as separate members with real economic interests.
   - Attorney and CPA review is needed to confirm gift, income-tax, grantor-trust, basis, accounting, creditor, and administration consequences.

5. Confirm whether single-member concern is actually the highest risk.
   - Lawsuits against BYH expose BYH assets regardless of member count.
   - Single-member concern is more about creditor remedies against the membership interest and how courts treat the entity in creditor, bankruptcy, or equitable-remedy situations.

## Documents That Need Review Or Update

- BYH operating agreement.
- BYH membership ledger / company records.
- Trust assignment of BYH membership interest.
- BYH manager/signing authority resolutions.
- Tax and accounting records showing member allocations.
- Any subsidiary operating agreements and ownership ledgers.
- Any estate-plan documents affected if personal or non-trust interests are created.
- If using two trusts, both trust instruments, both trust funding assignments, BYH ownership ledger, and BYH operating agreement provisions for two trust members.

## Working Recommendation

Do not add 1% interests or split the trust structure until the attorney and CPA review the tradeoff. The cleaner first step is to update BYH's operating agreement and records to reflect the current Trust as owner and separate manager/signing authority from member voting rights. If a second member is still desired, the two-trust option may be cleaner than personal 1% interests because it could preserve trust-based succession while creating two real members, but it must be documented as a genuine two-member structure with separate trust funding and LLC governance.
