# CFPB Consumer Contract-for-Deed Source Note

## Source

- Title: What is a contract for deed?
- Publisher: Consumer Financial Protection Bureau
- URL: https://www.consumerfinance.gov/ask-cfpb/what-is-a-contract-for-deed-en-2149/
- Last reviewed: 2024-09-13
- Captured: 2026-06-05

## Status

Background source for consumer-facing risk framing. Use for operational awareness, not as controlling legal guidance.

## Key Points For This Project

- CFPB describes contracts for deed as loans where the seller keeps legal title until payments are completed.
- Buyers may be responsible for taxes, insurance, repairs, and maintenance during the contract term.
- CFPB identifies common risks involving balloon payments, liens, taxes, insurance, repairs, and loss of buyer investment after default.

## Use In Drafting

Use this source to make sure the creditworthiness process accounts for the full monthly burden on the buyer, not just principal and interest.
