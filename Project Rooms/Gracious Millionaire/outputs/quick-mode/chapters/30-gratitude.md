# Gratitude

Have a kind and generous spirit because gratitude is a secret to all magnificent success.

That is not just a nice sentence.

It is a way of seeing.

Grace and gratitude come from the same Latin word, *gratia*, meaning favor, esteem, regard, a pleasing quality, goodwill, and from earlier roots meaning to announce, praise, or celebrate. In English, its first meaning was like divine favor, love, or assistance, typically unmerited assistance, but freely given anyway.

That matters because gratitude is not merely politeness.

Gratitude is agreement with grace.

When I am grateful, I am acknowledging that I have received something I did not manufacture by myself. I may have worked. I may have studied. I may have taken risks. I may have built systems. But gratitude reminds me that I am not the source of every good thing in my life.

Every word, every thought, every action, every ambition either comes out of love or comes out of fear. Everything is governed by one of those two influences.

The abiding of fear is the rejection of love.

That sentence challenges me.

When I am afraid, I can make decisions that look wise but are really self-protective. I can call it prudence when it is unbelief. I can call it strategy when it is fear. I can call it urgency when it is pressure. Gratitude interrupts that.

Prosperity cannot be the goal. Jesus said, I will add a hundred times what you left. Then He adds, with persecution.

The blessings of the Lord breed entitlement in the people of God if they do not remain humble and practice discipline.

That is the challenge of blessing.

He is more concerned with what I become internally than what I experience externally.

Ruling means to serve. Every position of authority has two basic reasons: to protect and to empower.

So if God gives us money, what is it for?

If God gives us properties, what are they for?

If God gives us influence with sellers, tenants, lenders, contractors, and attorneys, what is that influence for?

It is not so I can feel important.

It is not so I can complain from a nicer chair.

It is so I can protect and empower what He has placed in my care.

Gratitude keeps abundance from becoming entitlement.

Gratitude keeps increase from becoming arrogance.

Gratitude also keeps reward in the right place. Bill makes the point that God rewards, but the reward belongs inside relationship, not outside it. If I seek reward without seeking Him, I am back to money replacing trust in God. If I seek Him, then reward can come without becoming my identity.

Gratitude keeps the millionaire gracious.

It also keeps the leader submitted.

An ungrateful leader eventually treats people like obstacles. A grateful leader remembers that every person in the story belongs to God before they belong to the transaction, the project, the plan, or the spreadsheet.
