# Blessing

All things work together for the good to those who love the Lord.

Sometimes God has to get our attention.

Blessing is one of those words that can become vague if it is not attached to a real story. It can mean a feeling. It can mean money. It can mean a closed deal. It can mean relief from pressure.

But in this manuscript I am trying to use it more carefully.

Blessing is not merely that something good happened to us. Blessing is the evidence of God's hand arranging, correcting, opening, protecting, and teaching while we are walking out an assignment.

I believe we had unusual favor when we pursued the Red Quartz property.

Everything went very smoothly in the beginning. When the property came to our attention from all of the opportunities we were developing in the day-to-day activity of working our system, things moved quickly.

The foreclosure was filed on January 25. We found it on January 27. We sent our first letter to addresses that were not receiving mail on February 1. Our first text and email messages started on February 4. After no responses, I started searching on Facebook for the owner on February 6.

Nicholas responded right away.

So from the first day the file was recorded at the courthouse to our first conversation with the seller was twelve days.

Here is the flavor of the first Facebook exchange.

I introduced myself and told Nicholas I was interested in buying the property on Red Quartz in Raleigh. I told him I could pay cash quickly and rescue it from foreclosure. It would not cost him anything, and I would take care of the details so it was not trouble for him.

He responded that the house was already in foreclosure and going to the courts. It was locked up, and he could not get into it.

I told him I understood, but I could still work with it if he was willing.

I promised I could get him some money.

That short exchange led to a phone call, and I arranged to meet him at a place of his choosing the next day. He chose Chick-fil-A. It was a rainy day, and as it happened, his first, second, and third choices were all closed because of Covid protocols.

We ended up at the library.

There I talked to him and his wife, called his sister in Virginia, and reached an agreement. That included signing a contract and a deed and giving him some earnest money.

There were some hiccups along the way that I will not go into here, because all in all it was a smooth process to make a deal with the sellers.

God favored us so much that on the very first day we made contact with the sellers, we also reached an agreement with friends we had been talking to for months about a private money loan for $100,000. That was enough to purchase the property, bring the existing loan out of foreclosure, and completely rehab the property.

This was a pretty incredible trail of circumstances that had little to do with our engineering them.

We did our part.

But they worked because of God's favor.

I expect that everyone can imagine us doing all of these steps with no results. I can think of many times when similar steps bore no fruit. The letter goes out. The text goes out. The search is made. The seller never answers. The money is not available. The timing does not line up. The deal does not come together.

That is why I want to remind myself of the scripture from Paul.

All things work together for good to them that love God, to them who are called according to His purpose.

That scripture is not a promise that every transaction will close.

It is not a promise that every foreclosure file will become a profit.

It is not a promise that every seller will say yes.

It is a promise about God working in the lives of those who love Him and are called according to His purpose.

That means the blessing is not only the property.

The blessing is the way God weaves timing, people, resources, pressure, rain, closed restaurants, libraries, family conversations, private money, and a foreclosure deadline into something we could not have arranged by ourselves.

This was not the end of the Red Quartz story.

But it was enough to show us that blessing is often more than an event. It is a trail.
