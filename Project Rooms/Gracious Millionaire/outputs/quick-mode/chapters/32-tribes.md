# Tribes

Working on something you believe in is much more satisfying than working for a paycheck.

This is the answer to the help shortage.

That thought may be short, but I think it belongs in this book because it points to something bigger than hiring.

People do not only want wages.

They want meaning.

They want to know that the work matters. They want to be part of something that has a reason beyond the next invoice or the next closing. A tribe is not created by slogans. It is created when people recognize that they are giving their energy to something they believe in.

Jenny and I are still learning how to build that.

The real estate business needs contractors, assistants, lenders, attorneys, buyers, tenants, sellers, and partners. But underneath all of that, it needs people who can feel the assignment. If the work is only about money, then the moment a better paycheck appears, the heart moves with it.

That brings the leadership question back one more time.

People may follow a paycheck for a while, but they do not become a tribe because I have a plan. They become a tribe when there is enough clarity, trust, humility, and shared purpose for people to believe their work has meaning.

That kind of leadership cannot be forced.

It has to be lived.

But if the work is about helping people, stewarding property, creating opportunity, rescuing value, and doing business under lordship, then something else becomes possible.

Not just a workforce.

A tribe.

That is still a seed thought.

But many chapters begin as seeds.

Maybe this whole book is one.
