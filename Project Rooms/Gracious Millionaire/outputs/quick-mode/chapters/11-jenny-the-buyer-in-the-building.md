# Jenny: The Buyer In The Building

After the condo remodeling was finished, I hired a woman who lived in the same building to clean before the realtor pictures and the showings that were supposed to follow.

That detail may seem small, but it became important.

She knew many of the absentee owners in the building because she cleaned for a number of them. She had relationships I did not have. She knew people connected to that property in a way I did not. I had hired her for one practical reason, but God used that connection for another.

She spoke to one of the owners about my condo being for sale.

And just like that, I had the buyer.

I do not mean the process suddenly became unimportant or that the stress before it did not matter. I mean that the answer came through a channel I could not have produced by calculation alone. I had remodeled the condo. I had listed it. I had waited through no showings and no offers. I had worried over the financial clock.

Then the buyer came through a woman in the building who knew another absentee owner.

That is the kind of thing a person remembers.

To someone else, it may sound like a normal real estate connection. Buildings have owners. Owners know people. A cleaning woman hears something and mentions it. A buyer appears. But when you are the person under pressure, the person afraid, the person praying and waiting, you know there is more to the story than a convenient contact.

I saw the kindness of God in it.

Not because I had done everything perfectly. I had not. Not because I had been fearless. I had not. Not because my research had been complete. It had not been. I saw His kindness because He made a way when I did not know how to make one.

That experience did not make me careless in future real estate decisions. If anything, it made me more aware of how much I did not know. It showed me that a person can be careful and still need mercy. It showed me that a person can make a mistake in what she fails to investigate and still be held by God. It showed me that waiting is not wasted just because it is uncomfortable.

The Garden City Beach condo taught me more than one lesson.

It taught me to look beyond the obvious numbers. Mortgage coverage is not the same thing as profitability. Rental income is not the whole equation. HOA dues, assessments, insurance, management costs, timing, and market conditions all matter.

It taught me that selling under pressure is very different from selling by choice.

It taught me that the market can change at the worst possible time.

And it taught me that God can use ordinary people in ordinary places to answer prayers that feel very large to the person praying them.

That is why I cannot tell this story as only a mistake or only a rescue. It was both a lesson and a mercy.

My first real estate experiences began with a conventional starter home in Northern Virginia and led through family pressure, mortgage stress, divorce, equity, an oceanfront condo, an investment miscalculation, a market collapse, fear, waiting, and finally a buyer I did not find by myself.

I still value safety. I still value research. I still value plans. I still want decisions to be honest, rational, and well thought out.

But I know now that safety is not found only in the plan.

Safety is found in belonging to the Lord while the plan is being tested.
