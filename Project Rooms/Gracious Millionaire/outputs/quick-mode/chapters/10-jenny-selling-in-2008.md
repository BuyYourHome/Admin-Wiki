# Jenny: Selling In 2008

The year was 2008.

Anyone who remembers real estate in 2008 understands what that means. The market collapsed. The subprime mortgage crisis shook the country. Home prices fell dramatically. People who had planned to sell discovered that buyers had vanished or changed. Financing was harder. Confidence was gone.

And there I was, caught in the crosshairs.

It was a good time to buy, not a good time to sell. But I felt I had no choice. The condo was costing me money. The HOA dues and assessments had changed the math. I had remodeled it. I had put it on the market. I needed a way out.

The problem was that the market did not care what I needed.

The condo sat.

No showings. No offers. The financial clock was ticking.

That kind of waiting is difficult for anyone, but it is especially difficult for a person who wants certainty. I wanted a plan. I wanted a buyer. I wanted a date. I wanted the situation resolved before it could do more damage. Instead, I had silence.

I did not have the education I have now. I did not have the experience I have now. I certainly did not have the confidence I have grown into in recent years. I was facing a problem larger than my preparation.

That is a frightening place to be.

I can look back now and describe it with more calm than I felt then. At the time, I was afraid. I knew enough to understand that the market was bad and that my need to sell did not make the property sellable. I knew I could not force a buyer to appear. I knew the costs were real. I knew the responsibility was mine.

But I also knew my Heavenly Father had me in His hand.

I cannot say I was not afraid. That would not be honest. Faith did not make me fearless. But fear did not mean I was abandoned. I was clinging to Him and to His promises. I believed He had a plan, even though I could not see it yet.

That is where waiting becomes spiritual.

Waiting is not my natural strength. I am anything but patient when the stakes are high and the outcome is unresolved. I want to know what is going to happen. I want to make the responsible move. I want to fix what can be fixed.

But there are seasons when all the responsible steps have been taken and the answer still has not arrived.

In those seasons, a person learns what she is really trusting.

I had trusted my calculations. I had trusted rental projections. I had trusted homework. I had trusted the idea that a remodeled oceanfront condo should be sellable. None of those things was wrong in itself, but none of them was enough to deliver me.

I had to wait for God to show me.

That was not easy. It was not sentimental. It was not the kind of waiting that looks peaceful from the outside. It was the kind of waiting where a person keeps returning to the Lord because she does not know where else to go.

Looking back, I can see that He was not late. But while I was living it, the waiting felt long.
