# Gracious Millionaire

## Introduction

How much money is too much money?

It is not an amount. It is when the desire for money replaces trust in God.

In writing this book we are coming from the point of view of what we believe the Bible teaches concerning the Kingdom of God and how it relates to our finances. It is our belief that the Kingdom touches every aspect of life.

There are many books written about success and prosperity. There are many good books, books from all kinds of perspectives, that teach how to obtain success. You probably should read them. We have read some of them, enjoyed them, and learned from them. We do not claim to be experts at anything.

This is not a how-to book. Maybe it is a how-to-be book.

This is our story of our adventures with real estate investment and the lessons we have learned, or are learning. Jenny and I have made a million dollars in a short period of time in real estate. The bulk of this book will be the stories of some of these real estate transactions. They are the life lessons that have been set before us to teach us about the Kingdom of God. We will be as honest as we can be about these stories and what we have learned.

At the time I am writing this, I am building a framework for this book. The framework is a set of Biblical principles that I want to hang stories upon that we have experienced in real estate investment. I know, while I am writing this, that I do not have all of these stories identified. I do not have all of these lessons learned. But while I am writing this I am praying that God will give the examples needed to adequately reveal the truths.

Favor is the attraction of God to you that releases an influence through you so other people are inclined to like, to trust, and to cooperate with you in the assignment God gave you.

In its Biblical form it is almost always related to your God-given assignment. Think of Joseph, David, Daniel, Esther, Nehemiah and Mary. These are the notable Bible characters where the word "favor" showed up.

Many people fall short of a divine encounter because they are satisfied with good theology. The word of God is an invitation to meet the person.

For these ideas to be very effective they have to transform from a philosophy to a lifestyle. It is the difference between head knowledge and heart knowledge.

The Christian principles that we are talking about here are not just philosophy. As long as they stay just a philosophy, they have a certain effectiveness but they fall short. For them to have their full effectiveness they have to manifest in the obedience of servanthood as it relates to Jesus Christ.

So what is the difference between manipulation, favor, prayer, and declarations?

That is one of the questions behind this book.

## Failures in Real Estate

There are periods before the success in real estate that Jenny and I are writing about in this book that were not successful at all.

That has to be said.

If we are going to talk honestly about favor, blessing, prosperity, abundance, and becoming a gracious millionaire, then it would not be fair to only tell the stories that worked. It would not be fair to tell the stories that make us look wise and leave out the ones that reveal how much we did not know. It would not be fair to talk about success without talking about failure.

Jenny and I have both had failed marriages. I am not going to go deeply into all of that here. There are parts of those stories that are too personal and too complicated for the purpose of this chapter. But before I met and married Jenny, my second marriage failed, and there were real estate failures inside that failure.

For this narration, I am only going to refer to my second wife as number two.

Somewhere during that season I got hold of some of the real estate educational material that I am still paying attention to now. I began to think about real estate the way those mentors were teaching. It was the beginning of my real estate education.

I had been a Christian all my life. I tried to walk in a way that was consistent with what I understood my relationship with God should look like. I had deep-rooted beliefs that started when I was a child. My parents brought me up well in the understanding of God, and I honor the way they taught me to relate to the Lord.

But there were aspects of walking with God that I understand now that I did not understand then.

I do not think I knew anything about walking in the favor of the Lord.

I think I knew a good bit about proper confession, but I do not think I was walking in it. I knew theology. I knew many of the right words. But I do not think those truths had transformed into the daily dependence that I am trying to describe in this book.

That is the context for this story.

My history with real estate did not start with success.

My first house was bought with my first wife in Florida. We bought that house just before Ashley and Brandon were born. I was self-employed in the sign business then, and I built a garage on the back side of the house where my shop was. I had operated that business for about eight years, I think.

But I felt like my life was not going in the direction I wanted it to go. At the age of 30, we signed the deed over to my first wife's parents, moved in with them, and I started to college full time. Actually, I started double time. I enrolled in the local community college and finished a two-year associate's degree in one year.

Then we moved to Gainesville, and I enrolled at the University of Florida.

Mechanical engineering was a hard degree. It took me two years to finish one year of college there while I was working part-time to pay the bills and keep up with my studies. After completing my third year of college, we ran out of financial aid. It looked like college was going to have to stop, and I was going to have to go to work full-time.

So we packed up and moved to North Carolina to get started in a new location where I might finish college part-time. That never really happened. I found work, and my career in computer programming continued to develop.

We bought a little farmhouse in Johnston County, an old beat-up house.

I used to say I burned that house down one splinter at a time because when I was remodeling and taking all the old wood out, it went on the fire in the back and was replaced, little by little, with new stuff.

That is the house I lost two times.

The first time was when number one divorced me and took the house. Then she moved out of it and would not sell it to me. She sold it to some third party. We had four kids at the time, and I had no house to share with them when I had custody. So I bought the house back from the lady she had sold it to.

That is the house I was living in when I married number two.

Real estate had not been my friend at this point in my life.

Still, I kept going. There were also three major real estate transactions that number two and I engaged in that, in the end, were total failures. I think if I knew then what I know now, they might not have been. But there is no way to know that. A proper story about my life as a real estate investor would not be complete without talking about them.

One transaction was a home for us to live in when we were in Raleigh. At that time I was traveling back and forth between Houston and Raleigh. I shared custody of my four children with my first wife, and when I was in Raleigh I would pick them up and they would live with me while I was there.

The house we bought had two floors. The first floor was finished with three bedrooms and two bathrooms, and it opened up to the roadside and the driveway. Underneath it was an unfinished basement that was the same size as the upstairs and opened out from the back of the property.

This became my first major renovation.

We designed and built four bedrooms, two bathrooms, and a kitchen in that open unfinished basement. It was quite a project, and I did almost all of it myself. I designed it and worked with a general contractor, but with a few exceptions I did the work. I did not do the HVAC or the plumbing, but I did almost everything else.

Once it was completed, the design allowed our family to move downstairs and rent the upstairs. The rent from the upstairs paid the mortgage on the property.

That part worked.

It was a very sound financial model. We effectively had no expense for our personal home in Raleigh. Looking back, I can see that there were things in that house that anticipated the way I would later think about real estate. Use what you have. Create income from the property. Let the house carry itself if it can.

That was a good idea.

But good ideas are not enough by themselves.

The second major transaction was the purchase of a large hotel in the Galveston area of Texas. Number two was from the Houston area and had a home there. During that marriage I traveled back and forth between Houston and Raleigh, with one foot in Texas and one foot in North Carolina. It was a very busy time of my life.

We bought a 200 room rundown hotel right off a major highway between Houston and Galveston. We bought it from a private owner. It was not operational when we bought it, but it had a lot of potential. It had a restaurant, a bar, and three conference rooms. Many of the rooms needed a lot of maintenance before they could be used.

We structured the purchase with some cash and a promissory note to the seller for the remainder of the purchase. I do not have the records from that acquisition now. Those records were maintained by my ex, and the purchase was made in entities that we had set up and that she controlled. My memory is a little fuzzy after all these years, so I will only speak in general terms about the money. I think the total purchase price was around $3 million.

When we bought it, we moved into one of the suites and began to live and breathe the operation of the hotel.

Everything had to be brought up to operational condition. We hired consultants to advise us on the restaurant and conference rooms. We catered food for events we were attracting for the conference facilities. We got a set of rooms operational and began to have guests in the hotel.

It was exciting.

I am not trying to give a lesson on operating a hotel. I have experience, but nothing to teach from that experience except humility. I am giving context and backstory to the story Jenny and I are telling now.

The hotel never made money.

As the financial pressure built, the stress in our marriage built with it. Eventually I made the decision, in conjunction with number two, to move back permanently to Raleigh where I could get a job and begin to stem the losses we had endured.

We eventually lost the investment.

The third major investment was five duplexes in Johnston County, North Carolina. They were purchased from one individual, and they were all occupied by what was then called Section 8 housing residents.

They were bought around the same time as the hotel acquisition. My plan was to manage them during my trips back and forth to Raleigh. I got quite an education in property management during that time.

But again, in the end, they were lessons from failure.

As my marriage with number two began to fall apart under the stress of the finances from all the investments, I made a deal with her to buy her out of the duplexes. The loans had been acquired in her name. So I created a partnership agreement and entity with my mom to pay back the investment number two had made, and we transferred the deeds into our name.

The divorce was initiated, and number two demanded that we sell the Raleigh house. I offered to buy it from her. I had a loan in place to do it. But she would have nothing to do with that and demanded that it be sold. She sold it and took the proceeds because she had invested the money.

As part of the divorce settlement with number two, she also took the Johnston County farmhouse that I had owned before our marriage and that was being rented out at the time. So I lost that house the second time in a second divorce.

I moved into a nearby apartment where I could house my children. We stayed there for a little over a year, until my children were old enough to move out except for my youngest.

Then I bought an RV that was big enough for me to live in permanently and still have room for my youngest son when he stayed with me. We parked the RV in the backyard of one of the duplexes, where I could keep a close eye on the tenants and manage the properties.

We kept the five loans in place and took ownership subject to the existing mortgages. For a while they cash flowed well enough to pay the expenses. But little by little I had trouble keeping them rented at the level needed to pay the mortgages. They started getting behind.

This was around the time of the 2008 mortgage crisis. The bank was not very tolerant of the delinquencies. Honestly, I did not have the experience or education to find ways to avoid the consequences.

One by one, the banks foreclosed on all of them.

Over a period of about five years, we had bought a hotel, a personal residence, five rental duplexes, and some other investment properties. By the end of that season, both houses were gone in the second divorce, the hotel investment was gone, the duplexes were gone, and the marriage had ended in failure.

There were a lot of things wrong, and I will not try to go into all of them. I do not know what would have happened if I had been walking in the same principles I am trying to talk about in this book. I cannot say that I was daily taking my needs to God. I cannot say that I was asking for His favor. There were other dynamics wrong in the marriage for sure.

But I can say this.

The difference between that season and this season is not that I am smarter now.

I am more educated. That is true.

I have more experience. That is true.

I have better systems. That is true.

But those things are not the centerpiece of this book.

The difference I can recognize is that now we are walking in the favor of the Lord, and then I was not. At least I was not walking in it the way I understand it now. I was trying to do real estate with ideas, energy, pressure, and ambition. Some of the ideas were good. Some of the structures were sound. But I was not living with the same daily dependence on God that I am trying to describe now.

That matters.

This is not a book about how to get rich by using God. That would be a horrible book.

This is a book about lordship.

This is a book about what happens when money, opportunity, favor, work, marriage, stress, and ambition all come under the question of who is really Lord.

In that earlier season, I had real estate ambition before I had real estate favor. I had education before I had assignment. I had confession before I had the kind of dependence that makes confession powerful. I had motion, but I do not know that I had peace.

That does not mean God was absent. I do not believe He was absent. He was with me in the loss, the divorce, the apartment, the RV, the foreclosures, and the rebuilding. But I did not know how to cooperate with Him in the way I am trying to cooperate with Him now.

So this chapter belongs near the beginning.

Before we talk too much about the favor that followed us into profitable transactions, we need to say that we have also known what it is to lose. Before we talk about the blessing, we need to say that not everything we touched turned to gold. Before we talk about being millionaires, we need to say that some of the education came through failure.

Failure does not disqualify the story.

Sometimes it tells you what the story is really about.

## Favor

Favor is something that I have been learning about deeply for about eight years. I should credit the person who has been the most influential to us in this regard. I started watching YouTubes of Lance Wallnau about eight years ago when I was in between jobs. Favor is something that you do not just have because of your position or experience. Education and expertise is not the key component of favor.

Remember, everything we are saying here pivots upon our relationship to the King of this Kingdom.

Jesus grew in wisdom and in stature and in favor with God and all the people. Luke 2:52.

It is just that I believe that from a Christian point of view, a Christian who is striving for the Kingdom of God, success, as you might define it, cannot and should not be the goal.

Let me back up and speak to the person who is not coming from this point of view. You can follow the principles of success books and have great success. However, if you are a Christian striving for the Kingdom of God, your success will be very limited if success, prosperity, wealth, fame, or influence is your goal.

Why is that?

Because God is more interested in what is happening to you internally than what is happening to you externally.

But how can you write a book titled *Gracious Millionaire* and not be teaching about success? Let me say that I do want to be successful. I even want to be wealthy. But when I put any of these goals ahead of God's purposes I have always found that God has another card to play.

Jenny and I have benefited greatly from favor. It would be easy to think that the effort we have put into educating ourselves and the industrious plan we have implemented are what have resulted in our success. Of course, this is partly true. I think it unlikely that we would have received any of the results we have had if we had not taken these steps.

However, these steps are not taken in a vacuum. Our mentors in real estate have not taught these steps in a vacuum. Their lessons are prefaced with leading from a servant's heart, being grateful in all things, and demonstrating that by personal example when we are around them.

I want to take a closer look at the role favor has had in our success.

Lance Wallnau's definition of favor is:

**The attraction of God to you, that releases an influence through you, so other people are inclined to like, trust and cooperate with you in the assignment God gave you.**

That is really a mouthful. I believe that this is the centerpiece of our success.

Of course there is nothing specific about real estate in that definition, or any other endeavor. It does not speak to a career or an inclination to do any particular thing. It speaks to the assignment that God gave you. That of course could change from day to day.

I want to be listening every day for what that assignment is. I hope that I am. I invite my Father to correct me. He does not really need my invitation but I like the posture of being correctable.

A scripture that I have been aware of most of my life is:

You will see your teacher with your own eyes. Your own ears will hear him. Right behind you a voice will say, "This is the way you should go," whether to the right or to the left. - Isaiah 30:20-21

This speaks to being led by God. We all have assignments. Perhaps many get ignored along the way. We should pay attention to the clues He gives us about our assignment. In the vocation that Jenny and I have chosen as real estate investors, we have assignments. We also have assignments that have nothing to do with real estate. Sometimes we have assignments in the context of real estate that really have nothing to do with real estate.

One day I was looking to acquire a particular piece of property. Our normal approach after identifying a property that looks inviting is to go knock on the door of the property. "Let's see what happens."

Of course I am wanting to meet the owner and see if they are interested in selling. My approach is usually humble. I knock on the door and then step back to assess how I am going to be received. I have found that this definition of favor usually follows me in these circumstances.

On this particular day, my assignment was apparently not to buy real estate. It was to meet and talk to the occupant of this house. He did not own the house but was the executor of the will of his fiancee who had owned the house. She had died and left him alone and despondent. He invited me to sit in his living room and in the course of our long conversation I told him what our interest was but also listened to him tell his story. He let me encourage him and pray for God's blessing on him.

I never heard from him again but I believe the assignment did him good. Without God's favor, I would not have been well received. He would not have let me into his house and opened up. I do not know what happened to him but I know that I fulfilled my assignment that day.

Sometimes my assignment leads to a purchase of a property. When you have a divine assignment you can approach every situation fearlessly with little concern about the outcome. Sometimes that outcome leads to very profitable situations.

## Blessing

**All things work together for the good to those who love the Lord.**

Sometimes God has to get our attention. I believe we had unusual favor when we pursued the Red Quartz property.

Everything went very smoothly in the beginning. When the property came to our attention from all of the opportunities that we were developing in the day to day activities of working our system, things moved pretty quickly.

The foreclosure was filed on Jan 25th. We found it on Jan 27th. We sent our first letter to addresses that were not receiving mail on Feb 1st. Our first text and email messages started on Feb 4th. After no responses I started searching on Facebook for the owner on Feb 6th. Nicholas responded right away.

So from the first day the file was recorded at the courthouse to our first conversation with the seller was 12 days.

Here is our initial conversation from Facebook messenger.

Wes:

**Hi Nicholas, my name is Wes Browning. I am interested in buying the property that you have some interest in. The property is on Red Quartz in Raleigh. I can pay cash quickly and rescue it from foreclosure. It will not cost you anything and I will take care of all of the details so that it is not trouble for you. Please contact me.**

Feb 6, 2022, 3:32 PM.

**It is already in foreclosure and going to the courts. The house is locked up and I can't get into it.**

Wes:

**I understand but I can still work with it if you are willing. I promise. I can get you some money.**

Nicholas:

**Ok, what needs to be done**

Wes:

**Can I meet you today?**

Nicholas:

**Unfortunately, not. I am going to work in 15 minutes**

Wes:

**Ok, Can you call me?**

Nicholas:

**Ok, what is your number**

After this short exchange on Facebook we talked on the phone and I arranged to meet him at a place of his choosing the next day. He chose to meet at Chick-fil-A. It was a raining day and, as it happened, Nic's first, second, and third choices were all closed because of Covid protocols. We ended up going to the library where I talked to him and his wife, called Nick's sister in VA, and reached an agreement which included signing a contract and a deed and giving him some earnest money.

There were some hiccups along the way that I will not go into because all in all it was a smooth process to make a deal with the sellers.

God favored us so much that on the very first day we made contact with the sellers we also reached an agreement with friends we had been talking to for months about a private money loan for $100,000. This was enough to purchase the property, bring the existing loan out of foreclosure, and completely rehab the property.

This was a pretty incredible trail of circumstances that had little to do with our engineering them. We did our part but they worked because of God's favor.

I expect that everyone could imagine us doing all of these steps with no results. I could outline many times when similar steps bore no results. So I want to remind ourselves of the scripture from Paul.

**All things work together for the good to them that love God, to them who are called according to His purpose. Romans 8:28**

This was not the end of this story or the lessons that it had to teach us.

## Assignment

Here is another example of an opportunity that we had. I think it illustrates both favor on our lives and that God directs our paths. I definitely believe that I heard a voice behind me letting me know which way to turn as I put one foot in front of another working through this assignment. I do remember declaring that the property owner would be receptive to what I had to say.

I believe he was.

Let me first give some context to this story.

1. We have used the same strategy for getting leads from the beginning.
2. We collect foreclosure files from the courthouse and approach sellers before they lose their house.
3. We try to identify HOA foreclosures and exclude them from our list as being unprofitable.
4. Sometimes we do not realize they are HOA foreclosures and contact them anyway.

Recently someone answered our campaign concerning their house. After I spoke to him I requested the file from the courthouse and realized the source was an HOA and that he owed less than $4,000. Our mentality was that no one is going to let the house go to foreclosure over $4,000. Therefore, there is no need to pursue it.

But this time something got my attention and I started looking to see how I could structure an appealing offer to the seller that would also be in our interest.

I did not yet know the details of his personal situation and certainly everyone's is different. I would come to know some of those details later, but before I knocked on his door, I considered a framework for a deal that would look like this.

1. A substantial cash offer for the deed.
2. Take his mortgage subject to.
3. Pay off the foreclosure lien.
4. Allow the owner to stay in the house and pay rent equal to his mortgage, taxes, insurance and HOA cost.

I arranged to meet the home owner. At this point I had sent him 16 letters that he had not responded to. However, I had just started a text campaign and he had received 3 text messages from me that he did respond to. Even though it was an HOA foreclosure, one that I would never have considered, I now had an assignment to go and talk to him.

I found him to be very cordial and inviting. He showed me through his house and then we started talking. I asked him if he wanted to sell his house. He started talking about big ideas. I did not really think they were reality based but I listened. He wanted to sell this house and get a bigger one with a yard for his kids. He showed me the small crowded, fenced-in back yard. I nodded to it all and then asked him if I could find a way to remove his difficulties and let them stay in the house, would he be interested?

I could see his interest perk up somewhat so I started finding out what I needed to know. I asked him for a current mortgage statement. He brought one out and showed me a current, reasonably small balance. I was intrigued so I started outlining the structure of an offer as I had outlined above. I then asked him what amount of cash he would need for him to sell me the house.

Sellers really never fully understand this kind of offer at first. They are looking for a bottom line number that they want me to give them for their house. He thought his house was worth something like $285,000 which I was never going to offer him. But through the conversation I began to explain and help him to see how my offer worked for him.

There is no need to give him a lot of details at first that are just going to get lost in the haze. I will explain them as he begins to grasp what I am offering and ask questions.

He did not immediately come up with the amount of cash that would make him want to accept this deal. So I gave him a low figure. I could see the margins on this house gave me plenty of wiggle room. I asked him to consider $10,000.

He started relating how someone else had made him an offer and started talking about his credit card balance and car payments. I came to realize that his other offer was really an offer to refinance his debt. I quickly reconsidered what I would offer. I rolled those balances into one cash offer of $40,000. He seemed interested.

He said, I could sell the house for more and I agreed.

I told him you could sell the house for more but then you would have to move and you would pay just as much for the next house. You could probably sell the house easily but in this market you would not, as easily, buy your next house. He would have to move immediately. I looked over at his wife who was listening and said, do you really want to move?

Again I outlined that he could pay off all of his debt and have the reduced payments to live on. He would have extra cash to use for whatever he wanted to. His family would not be disrupted with finding a new house and moving during the school year. And he would be out of trouble, with the prospect of losing his house, with the HOA.

I then thought to ask him what he made in a year. He answered that he made $42,000 per year. I said, I am offering you a year's salary and allowing you to stay in your house as long as you want to.

That is the flavor of that appointment with this home owner.

At this point, I have not heard back from him. But I went back and made some small changes to our Project Management spreadsheet to make a better analysis of this strategy. Once I had spent an hour on it I realized that I had discovered a new gold mine.

This was going to open up a great new source of leads that had a huge profit potential.

There would be some homeowners that would find this offer to be very tempting. What it provided for us was the ability to get into a new tenant occupied property that was going up, currently, 20% per year. These properties need no rehab, so there would only be the immediate expense of the HOA foreclosure redemption and the smallish cash offer. We were not going to have positive cash flow but we had an immediate amount of equity that was several times bigger than our investment. We could then borrow that equity to do another deal. The real kicker was that we were gaining the amount of equity that the property appreciated every year.

And we had a tenant that treated the property like his own home.

My spreadsheet showed the current profit matrix on this property to be:

- 12 months = $199,078
- 24 months = $257,798
- 36 months = $316,510

We would also have the immediate ability to borrow $103,500 on the property to finance other deals.

This assignment may not have been about this home owner. Rather, it may have been to open our eyes to other deals that we were letting pass us by.

There is so much room in this structure for profitability that we can be flexible with the next home owner. We can be flexible with the amount of cash offered the seller. We could lower the rent below their current mortgage payment because we can make up for it with the new loan that we could get. There is even the possibility of a lease to purchase contract allowing the seller to buy their property back in the future.

Somewhere soon we will find a property and home owner that is going to consider this to be a big win for themselves.

## 115 Rosebrooks

There is a kind of favor that feels wonderful when it first arrives. Doors open. People respond. A complicated thing becomes simple. A distressed property becomes an assignment. You can look back and say, "That was God."

But favor is not just about getting a deal.

Favor is the attraction of God to you that releases an influence through you so other people are inclined to like, trust, and cooperate with you in the assignment God gave you. I still believe that. But I have also learned that the assignment does not always end when the paperwork is signed. Sometimes the assignment continues after the blessing becomes complicated. Sometimes it continues when the person you helped later becomes the person whose name is on the other side of the lawsuit.

That is part of what 115 Rosebrooks became for us.

The property was in Cary. The owner, Brynda Griffith, was in foreclosure. Bank of America had started foreclosure proceedings in September of 2023. She had received notices. She understood enough to know that she could lose the house if something did not happen.

We were working the same system we had worked many times before. We monitor foreclosure filings, primarily in Wake and Johnston County, looking for opportunities where we may be able to buy a property and also solve a problem. We sent letters. She received letters from others too. Later the record would say she called us because our letters seemed the most professional.

I do not know how to make that sound more spiritual than it is. We sent letters. She called.

That is often how favor looks from the outside.

On October 28, 2023, Brynda called me. We spoke briefly and arranged to meet. The next day I went to the property. She did not want to talk in front of the neighbors, so we went somewhere nearby and talked through the situation. I asked what I usually ask. What do you need from me? Do you want to sell? Do you want to move? Do you need something else?

She did not have the money to stop the foreclosure. She did not have anyone stepping in to help her. She did not have somewhere else she wanted to go.

So the structure that emerged was a sale and leaseback. Buy Your Home would buy the property for the amount of the debt, and she could stay in the home at a very low rent. The rent was tied to the annual insurance premium. The lease had an initial five-year term and was renewable by her. In plain language, the intent was that she could stay as long as she wanted, if that is what she wanted.

That was the generosity inside the structure.

On October 31, 2023, we went over the paperwork. The documents included the purchase agreement, the lease, a seller acknowledgement, and a limited power of attorney so I could communicate with Bank of America if needed to stop the foreclosure. The seller acknowledgement said the transaction was a sale and not a loan. It said she had been advised to seek independent legal and financial counsel. It said she understood she remained liable on the mortgage until the debt was paid in full and that a default could still subject her to foreclosure. It said she was satisfied with the agreement.

I know paperwork does not make the heart pure. I know it does not answer every moral question. But I also know that graciousness without clarity can become confusion later. If you are going to do business in distressed situations, you must learn to be both compassionate and clear.

That is a hard balance.

On November 1, 2023, the Wake County clerk authorized the foreclosure sale. On November 15, I paid Bank of America $6,266.78 to cure the arrears. I did not have to use the power of attorney to do it. On November 16, Bank of America filed notice that the foreclosure would not proceed.

The house was saved from foreclosure.

That sentence should have felt like the end of the story. It was not.

For the next year, Brynda continued living in the home. We did more than own the house. Jenny and I visited. I drove Brynda to medical appointments. I helped with groceries. I helped with technology. We checked on her. I believed we were doing what we had said we would do. I believed we had made a business decision that also carried mercy inside of it.

This is one of the tensions of being a gracious millionaire.

If you are poor and generous, people may call you kind.

If you are prosperous and generous, people may ask what you are getting out of it.

Sometimes that question is fair. Sometimes it is wise. Money gives people power, and power has to be examined. But the existence of profit does not automatically cancel the existence of love. A business can make money and still help someone. A transaction can create equity and still keep a person from losing everything. A deal can be profitable and still be merciful.

The religious mind often wants those categories separated. Either it is charity or it is business. Either it is ministry or it is investment. Either it is love or it is profit.

I do not think the Kingdom is that small.

Stewardship is the proper use of what God has given us in the way that He would have us use it. He has not given us possessions only for our own good, but for work we can do for others. If that is true, then the question is not whether money is involved. The question is whether Jesus is Lord over the money.

That question became much harder when the lawsuit came.

In September of 2024, Brynda's daughter came to North Carolina. Shortly after that, Brynda was taken to Maryland. The next communication was not gratitude or a question or a conversation. It was a summons and complaint.

The lawsuit tried to unwind the transaction. It claimed fraud, breach of fiduciary duty, unfair and deceptive trade practices, undue influence, and lack of mental capacity. At first, the daughter had herself appointed guardian ad litem. That order was later set aside. Brynda then proceeded on her own behalf.

I am not writing this as a legal argument. Lawyers can do that better than I can. The legal record has its own language and its own rules. I am writing this because the spiritual pressure of the moment matters to the story of this book.

I had to ask myself what graciousness looks like when it is accused.

It is easy to be gracious when the other person is thankful. It is easy to talk about favor when the deal works and everybody smiles. It is much harder when the story is retold in a way that makes your help sound predatory. It is much harder when the person who received the benefit is now the plaintiff. It is much harder when your wife is hurt because she knows the groceries, the rides, the visits, and the care that do not show up neatly in a complaint.

Jenny said, in substance, that we had helped this woman in a big way.

That was how it felt to us.

But feelings are not enough either. Being hurt does not make us right. Being accused does not make us innocent. Being generous does not mean we never need correction. I have tried to keep my heart open to that. I have invited my Father to correct me before, and I have to keep inviting Him when the correction would be expensive or embarrassing.

Still, I also had to resist the temptation to let bitterness write the next chapter.

There was a moment when our attorney talked through the economic side of settlement. Maybe the practical answer was to buy out the lease and stop the bleeding. Maybe the principled answer was to fight. Maybe those two roads could not be walked at the same time. That was hard for me because I wanted the best possible settlement, but I also knew that if a reasonable settlement did not come, I was prepared to go to the wall.

That is not always faith. Sometimes it is anger wearing a good suit.

So I had to sit with Psalm 37.

Do not fret. Trust in the Lord and do good. Commit your way to Him. Rest in the Lord. Cease from anger. The steps of a good man are ordered by the Lord. The righteous shows mercy and gives.

Those words are easy until you are paying lawyers.

Those words are easy until a public lawsuit is attached to your name.

Those words are easy until you are tempted to explain yourself to people who have already decided what kind of person you are.

But the Kingdom does not stop being true because the assignment becomes painful.

In fact, this may be where the theme of this book becomes more honest. A gracious millionaire is not just someone who gets rich and stays nice. That is too thin. A gracious millionaire is someone who lets money remain under lordship even when money is being used as evidence against him. He does not let prosperity become entitlement. He does not let accusation become hatred. He does not let the need to win replace the need to remain faithful.

I wish I could say I have done that perfectly.

I have not.

But I can say that 115 Rosebrooks has forced the question deeper into me. Do I want to be vindicated more than I want to be obedient? Do I want to be right more than I want to be clean? Do I want the property, the profit, the reputation, or the peace of knowing that I stayed under the hand of God?

The answer is supposed to be obvious.

It is not always obvious when the pressure is real.

That is why abundance is dangerous. The blessings of the Lord can breed entitlement in the people of God if they do not remain humble and practice discipline. The more breakthrough we get, the more opposition we get. Jesus promised a hundredfold, but He also said, with persecution.

I used to read that as a warning.

Now I read it as part of the contract.

The lesson of 115 Rosebrooks is not finished. The legal story may still have more pages. The financial result may still change. There may be things we learn that we do not yet know. But I know the chapter belongs in this book because it shows the part of graciousness that does not feel gracious.

It shows that favor is not a formula.

It shows that generosity has to be governed by wisdom.

It shows that paperwork matters, clarity matters, and compassion matters.

It shows that profit is not proof of guilt and kindness is not proof of wisdom.

It shows that when the enemy comes in like a flood, the Spirit of the Lord lifts up a standard. Sometimes that standard is not a dramatic victory. Sometimes it is a quiet refusal to complain. Sometimes it is continuing to trust God with your name, your money, your reputation, and your unfinished story.

I still believe we were assigned to 115 Rosebrooks.

I also believe the assignment was not only to buy a house.

It was to learn what kind of heart can be trusted with houses.

## Providence Landing

Without any doubt Providence Landing was the biggest project we have ever attempted. It is a long story with lots of lessons, some unfinished.

To begin the story, I will have to make a confession.

It is nothing I am proud of, but the story cannot be told without it.

Jenny and I were fighting about some aspects of the business. Our conversation had gotten heated, and I told her that I was going to get away for a few days because I needed a break from our relationship. I hurriedly packed a few things in a bag, grabbed my computer and headed out somewhere without a plan. I was clear headed enough to tell her I would be back but that was about all that I saw clearly.

Pulling out of our driveway and our neighborhood, I allowed many thoughts to pass through my brain about what I was going to do. One of the thoughts that gelled was that I had been communicating, via Facebook messenger, with a guy in Virginia that had about 80 acres for sale. I was frequently looking for acreage that we could buy the way that we buy houses. In other words, buy with terms that we could easily afford.

What was I going to do with the property?

I do not know, but one idea that I had been thinking about for several months was to build an airpark. I will explain what that means later.

I set my sights on Virginia and started trying to reach the property owner. Looking back at the text was some GPS coordinates. I will not bore you with the details of how I found the property, but that information was not sufficient. I did find the property and talked to the owner who gave me a tour of what he owned.

It had been pouring rain the whole day since I left Raleigh. It had let up some as the owner drove me around his property. I was attracted to it and the price was not bad, but I concluded quickly that it was not going to serve the purpose of an airpark. I thanked him and left the area as the rain intensified.

Driving around, aimlessly, in some fierce storms, I decided to find a hotel in South Boston, Va. I got some groceries and went to the hotel to get out of my wet clothes, take a shower, and relax. My attitude was horrible, my disposition was depressed, as was my energy level. I had not treated my wife well and there was really nothing about my response that I was proud of.

I was just going through the motions of what I knew to do. I went to bed that way and woke up that way. After a morning shower and breakfast, I fell back on the bed and looked up at the ceiling, expecting to see God there.

Going through the motions sometimes works.

My lifetime habit is to look to God even when I am off track with his best expectations for my life. I looked at the ceiling and said out loud, **"God I do not know if you can speak to me but I am listening."**

Hearing nothing, after a few minutes, I reengaged with my motions, those ones that I was going through. I walked over to my laptop and started searching for acreage in southern Virginia.

## Abundance

So shall they fear The name of the LORD from the west, And His glory from the rising of the sun; When the enemy comes in like a flood, The Spirit of the LORD will lift up a standard against him. Isaiah 59:19-20.

What God is wanting from us is lordship.

He provides abundance but it looks different for every person. Jesus said the kingdom of God is within you. So all kingdom issues are heart issues. And all heart issues have to do with lordship.

There are three stories Jesus provides that show lordship does not look the same for each of us. He does not demand the same circumstances from all of us, except that he is our Lord.

The rich young ruler was missing one thing. He was told to sell everything he had and give to the poor.

Zacchaeus, walking with Jesus, having a conversation, said he would give half.

Peter said he had left all to follow Jesus. Jesus' response was Mark 10: you shall receive a hundred times as much, in this life, for what you left, with persecution.

We must be faithful in little to be trusted with much. The more breakthrough we get, the more opposition we get.

Our love for God is measured by how we love people.

Our love for God must also be seen by how we take care of things in this world that he has provided.

We claim spiritual realities but they have to be measurable by how we take care of things in the natural world.

All the parables about money had to do with his expectation of increase.

You must learn to steward what we have in the beginning, which is small, into something greater. Stewardship is the proper use of what God has given us in the way that he would have us use it. He has not given us possessions for our own good but for work we can do for others.

The story of the landowner paying wages pays the same to him who worked one hour as the one who worked 12 hours, but all for what they agreed to. We have generosity, contentment, and excellence with what we have. We have to know how to handle what we have been provided.

## Gratitude

Have a kind and generous spirit because gratitude is a secret to all magnificent success.

Grace and gratitude come from the same Latin word *gratia*, meaning favor, esteem, regard, a pleasing quality, goodwill, and from earlier roots meaning to announce, saying praise, celebrate. In English its first meaning was like divine favor, love, or assistance, typically unmerited assistance by the way, but freely given anyway.

Every word, every thought, every action, every ambition either comes out of love or comes out of fear. Everything is governed by one of those two influences.

The abiding of fear is the rejection of love.

Prosperity cannot be the goal. Jesus said, I will add 100 times what you left. Then he adds, with persecution.

The blessings of the Lord breed entitlement in the people of God if they do not remain humble and practice discipline.

He is more concerned with what I become internally than what I experience externally.

Ruling means to serve. Every position of authority has two basic reasons: to protect and to empower.

That is the challenge of blessing.

## Complaining

God's anger is always aimed at what interferes with love.

Complaining is the language of unbelief.

When the lordship of Jesus is compromised, complaining will be the evidence.

Nobody complains who sees God's role in the middle of their situation.

Complaining is almost always reasonable. All you have to do is remove the presence and promises of God to make it legitimate.

If you cannot trust God with lack you will never trust him with abundance.

Complaining distorts your view of reality.

Sometimes when God withholds clarity, it is his mercy because he knows our bent to fabricate formulas to replicate success instead of relying upon him, moment to moment, for inspiration for what we are doing.

A man will be satisfied with good by the fruit of his mouth.

The measure for what I receive is set by what comes out of my mouth.

Some things have to be said.

I will say of the Lord, "He is my refuge and my fortress, my God, in whom I trust." - Psalms 91

Things come into my life when they come out of my mouth.

He inhabits the praise of his people.

If God occupies my praise, who occupies my complaints?
