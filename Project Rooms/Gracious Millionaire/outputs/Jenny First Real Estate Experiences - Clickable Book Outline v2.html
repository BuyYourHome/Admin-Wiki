Placement In The Whole Manuscript

This Jenny section is inserted after `Always God Is There` and before Wes's `Failures in Real Estate`.

That placement keeps Jenny's first real-estate experiences separate from Wes's chapters while preserving the broader property chronology:

  • Jenny's first conventional home purchase in 1978.
  • Jenny's family-home pressure and equity story through the first marriage.
  • Jenny's Garden City Beach condo investment beginning after her 2002 move to Raleigh.
  • Jenny's 2008 market-collapse selling season.
  • Wes's real-estate failures and later property sequence after that.

Section Purpose

Jenny's section gives the reader her real-estate formation in her own voice before the book moves into Wes's real-estate failure, favor, and property chapters.

The section should not make Jenny sound like an obstacle to vision. It should show her caution as part of the book's wisdom: safety, research, planning, integrity, stewardship, fear, waiting, and trust all belong in the story.

Manuscript Chapters Created

### Jenny's Introduction: The Safe Way

Introduces Jenny's own voice and natural orientation: safety, security, research, order, caution, high standards, and predictable plans. Frames caution as something that can be wisdom or fear, depending on whether it is submitted to trust.

### Jenny: The Conventional Way

Covers Jenny's first home purchase in Northern Virginia in 1978: marriage at 23, apartment living, two jobs each, saving for a down payment, buying a $37,000 starter home with a conventional bank mortgage, and the dignity of ordinary responsibility.

### Jenny: Trading The Sports Car For A Truck

Covers the early-family transition: wanting children, choosing for Jenny to stay home, selling the 260Z sports car to buy a truck for Number One's remodeling business, saving a full year of Jenny's paychecks to test one-income living, and later recognizing God's honor in what felt like good planning.

### Jenny: Three Babies Under Two

Covers the arrival of a son and then twin girls, outgrowing the starter home and family car, buying the $99,000 larger home, buying the Dodge Ram van, and carrying mortgage and vehicle pressure in a high-interest-rate season.

### Jenny: Equity And Loss

Covers the ongoing cycle of falling behind and catching up, the pressure inside the first marriage, the eventual divorce after 25 years, selling the house for several hundred thousand dollars over what was owed, and the tension that financial equity can grow while a marriage weakens.

### Jenny: The Beach Condo Investment

Covers Jenny's 2002 move to Raleigh, investing home-sale proceeds into a Garden City Beach oceanfront condo, calculating rental income and management costs, seeing the first summer cover a year of mortgage payments, and discovering that rising HOA dues and assessments had not been counted deeply enough.

### Jenny: Selling In 2008

Covers remodeling and listing the condo in the 2008 market collapse, the lack of showings or offers, the financial clock ticking, the difference between education then and now, and the hard waiting of a cautious person whose plan is not working visibly.

### Jenny: The Buyer In The Building

Covers the buyer coming through the woman in the building who cleaned for Jenny and knew absentee owners. Closes the first section by naming the lesson: careful planning matters, but it cannot control everything; fear can rise while faith is present; God can provide through ordinary relationships already near the problem.
