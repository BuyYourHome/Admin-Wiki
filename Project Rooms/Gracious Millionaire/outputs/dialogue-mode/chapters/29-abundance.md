# Abundance

**Narrator:** This chapter is written as a crafted Wes-and-Jenny dialogue from project-room source material. It is not a transcript. The conversation goal is to let Wes and Jenny examine the lesson carried by Abundance.

**Wes:** 1. The rich young ruler was missing one thing. He was told to sell everything he had and give to the poor.

**Wes:** 2. Zacchaeus, walking with Jesus, having a conversation, said he would give half.

**Jenny:** When you say that, I hear both faith and pressure. Where did caution need to speak into it?

**Wes:** 3. Peter said he had left all to follow Jesus. Jesus' response was Mark 10: you shall receive a hundred times as much, in this life, for what you left, with persecution.

**Wes:** We must be faithful in little to be trusted with much. The more breakthrough we get, the more opposition we get.

**Wes:** The story of the landowner paying wages pays the same to him who worked one hour as the one who worked 12 hours, but all for what they agreed to. Shine for justice. Do not for extreme grace.

**Jenny:** What part of that lesson still feels unfinished for you?

**Wes:** We have generosity, contentment, and excellence with what we have. We have to know how to handle what we have been provided.

**Wes:** Our love for God is measured by how we love people.

**Wes:** Our love for God must also be seen by how we take care of things in this world that he has provided.

**Jenny:** How do we tell that story without making success sound like the proof that every decision was right?

**Wes:** We claim spiritual realities but they have to be measurable by how we take care of things in the natural world.

**Wes:** 4. All the parables about money had to do with his expectation of increase.

**Jenny:** Where did family peace belong in that decision?

**Wes:** You must learn to steward what we have in the beginning, which is small, into something greater. Stewardship is the proper use of what God has given us in the way that he would have us use it. He has not given us possessions for our own good but for work we can do for others.
