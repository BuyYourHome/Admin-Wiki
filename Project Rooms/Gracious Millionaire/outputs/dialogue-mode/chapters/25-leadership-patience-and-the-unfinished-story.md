# Leadership, Patience, And The Unfinished Story

**Narrator:** This chapter is written as a crafted Wes-and-Jenny dialogue from project-room source material. It is not a transcript. The conversation goal is to hold leadership, waiting, and Jenny's caution together without forcing resolution.

**Wes:** 1. This is a story about lessons identified but still being worked out. I can’t say I have learned them.

**Wes:** 2. I would like to identify these kind of lessons in training, as well as clear lessons that have been learned in each chapter

**Jenny:** When you say that, I hear both faith and pressure. Where did caution need to speak into it?

**Wes:** 3. For now, This chapter will stand on its own

**Wes:** 4. Leadership, I uploaded a video from bill Johnson who’s teaching I would like to draw on and apply here.

**Wes:** 5. I am convinced that leadership in not manipulation. Or to be softer, its not even about being so persuasive as to bring others to your point of view all the time

**Jenny:** What part of that lesson still feels unfinished for you?

**Wes:** 6. Many times it seeing a solution to a problem, mapping it out, explaining it to others and then waiting patiently while other do not understand or agree with your vision

**Wes:** 7. Lets use this book as an example. At this point I have created about 140 pages of manuscript. Jenny is interested, she is involved in the stories, she has a point of view that is often divergent from mine. However, she has not yet contributed by writing anything.

**Wes:** 8. I have a process of writing a little bit of a subject and sending it to Jean who is constantly integrating the topics into a coherent story

**Jenny:** How do we tell that story without making success sound like the proof that every decision was right?

**Wes:** 9. I would like for Jenny to have been doing the same the whole time. Our points of view would have merged into a conversational story of individual and mutual lessons learned

**Wes:** 10. She will, I am sure, eventually contribute a lot. She has a very substantial story to tell.

**Wes:** 11. So far she hasn’t done that . And so, at this point, the book is not a collaborative effort

**Jenny:** Where did family peace belong in that decision?

**Wes:** 12. My leadership vison believes she will become collaborative. However, I can’t make her write and any effort in that direction would be counter-productive.

**Wes:** 13. I am writing this chapter now but I think that when she gets involved, the flavor of it will change

**Wes:** 14. I want to have the benefit of her contribution now, but that isn’t how she works and leadership demands that I wait and let it unfold naturally

**Jenny:** When you say that, I hear both faith and pressure. Where did caution need to speak into it?

**Wes:** 15. The same leadership traits, or need for them, is evident in the unfinished story of the Orphaned Property.

**Wes:** 16. That story hasn’t finished yet and it is frustrating to me.

**Wes:** 17. If I were to run with my thoughts on the matter, I would conclude that she has blown up the deal. We had a willing buyer who wanted to trade some property for equity in our property.

**Jenny:** What part of that lesson still feels unfinished for you?

**Wes:** 18. I had done a lot of documentation, analysis and presentation That would have been used to present a proposal and create closing documents. It was a profitable, timely deal ready to happen.

**Wes:** 19. But Jenny did not want the properties and I could not convince her otherwise. I could have strong willed my way to making an offer but she would never have been in agreement

**Wes:** 20. So when I it came time to meet the buyer with his English speaking associate, I had no room to negotiate in that direction and that seemed to be are requirement of his.

**Jenny:** How do we tell that story without making success sound like the proof that every decision was right?

**Wes:** 21. Left to my own, I would have probably found enough common ground to make a deal but that didn’t happen.

**Wes:** 22. What am I left with. Is it resentment because we lost a deal that she didn’t want

**Wes:** 23. The truth is I usually think I am right about such things. But so does she. I still value her opinion even when it is hard for me to show it because I don’t agree with it.

**Jenny:** Where did family peace belong in that decision?

**Wes:** 24. One thing I know is that when caution is wrong it is hard to prove. That is not true when I am wrong, the evidence is laying all around me.

**Wes:** 25. If she is wrong, the mistake is not fatal. But overriding her point of view may have grave consequences, still probably not fatal

**Wes:** 26. I think Leadership allows events to unfold patiently without reacting. This is especially true for the Christian who actual believes that “All things work together for the good of those who love the Lord”.

**Jenny:** When you say that, I hear both faith and pressure. Where did caution need to speak into it?

**Wes:** 27. Maybe her slowness to begin writing is the correct path.

**Wes:** 28. Maybe her resistance to the selling the Orphaned property is for our good!

**Wes:** 29. I don’t know, but I know that I don’t know

**Jenny:** What part of that lesson still feels unfinished for you?
