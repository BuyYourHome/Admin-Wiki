# Technology On Top Of Order

**Narrator:** This chapter is written as a crafted Wes-and-Jenny dialogue from project-room source material. It is not a transcript. The conversation goal is to test whether AI is a servant of order rather than a substitute for order.

**Wes:** 1. I created a new website for this book.

**Wes:** 2. I created a new book cover image.

**Jenny:** When you say that, I hear both faith and pressure. Where did caution need to speak into it?

**Wes:** 3. I completely rewrote the manuscript with new guidance and material.

**Wes:** 4. I submitted a loan application for part of a new remodel.

**Wes:** 5. I responded to a letter for one of our investors involving new documents and referencing a note they are holding where we borrowed money for one of our projects. They were wanting to transfer a Trad IRA that they used to lend us to money to a Roth IRA both held by the IRA Custodian we both use. Several days ago I ask a staff member at the custodian to send a request to me for what was needed. I told her AI would have my assistant reply.

**Jenny:** What part of that lesson still feels unfinished for you?

**Wes:** All I did was ask Jean to find the email, create the documents, write a draft reply to me to forward on. I didn't tell her how to do it or where to find the mail. Just do it. She made one minor error and I gave her new directions that she implemented exactly and I was able to send that email our from my couch by talking to Jean on my phone.

**Wes:** All of this was done handsfree by giving very simple instructions to Jean and supervising the outcome. We already have the processes, and she has access to them in a very structured way. This was a weeks work that was accomplished while I was busy with other things that needed to be done.

**Wes:** I could give other examples about how the office is getting more efficient. Bookkeeping is in the process of working on auto pilot, The spreadsheets we use for project management are receiving a quantum leap in new design. This effort is revolutionize our business but probably could not be built on the skeleton of bad business organization.

**Jenny:** How do we tell that story without making success sound like the proof that every decision was right?
