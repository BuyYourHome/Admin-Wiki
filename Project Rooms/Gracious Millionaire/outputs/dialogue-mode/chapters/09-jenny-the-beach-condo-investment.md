# Jenny: The Beach Condo Investment

**Narrator:** This chapter is written as a crafted Wes-and-Jenny dialogue from project-room source material. It is not a transcript. The conversation goal is to let Wes and Jenny examine the lesson carried by Jenny: The Beach Condo Investment.

**Jenny:** I moved to Raleigh, NC in 2002, and took the proceeds from the home sale and invested much of it into buying an ocean front condo in Garden City Beach, SC, south of Myrtle Beach. I carefully calculated how the condo would rent out to vacationers, how much the HOA fees were, insurance, etc., and hired an onsite management company to take care of the rest. They charged me 40%! Rest assured, I had done my homework, and 40% was the going rate up and down the beach.

**Jenny:** I had no intention of ever living there, it was only an investment. That first summer, the rentals paid for an entire year of mortgage payments, and I thought I was on my way. In all my careful research and planning, I had failed to investigate how/if the HOA dues, and assessments might increase, and within 3-4 years, those fees had put me in the red, even though the rental monies easily paid each and every one of my mortgage payments. I guess you could say I panicked.

**Wes:** What do you want the reader to understand about what that cost you emotionally?

**Jenny:** This was all on me, and I didn’t see a way to stay afloat. I got a contractor to completely remodel the condo, and I placed it on the market for sale.
