# Providence Landing: Letting Go

**Narrator:** This chapter is written as a crafted Wes-and-Jenny dialogue from project-room source material. It is not a transcript. The conversation goal is to let Wes and Jenny examine the lesson carried by Providence Landing: Letting Go.

**Wes:** 1. It's been over 2 years since the time I decided to shut down the effort to develop Providence landing.

**Wes:** 2. I made an appointment with Jen and Robbie to drive up so I could tell them face-to-face that it was no further efforts I could make on the project

**Jenny:** When you say that, I hear both faith and pressure. Where did caution need to speak into it?

**Wes:** 3. It is very difficult to look back at this. Fully understand what happened.

**Wes:** 4. I was so fully convinced that God had directed me to the property and guided my thoughts about the development of the project

**Wes:** 5. I spent an enormous amount of time developing the plans, pursuing relationships, strategizing with the team that I had built. It was hard to let go of it all

**Jenny:** What part of that lesson still feels unfinished for you?

**Wes:** 6. One thing that I can see looking back with this much distance behind me is I don't know how I would have pulled it off if we had gotten the funding and dove into the full development of it. I don't know what it would have done to Jenny and I in the stress that would have been involved.

**Wes:** 7. We met some great people. Hopefully we'll be able to continue the relationships that we built

**Wes:** 8. I won't explain much about one relationship that Jenny and I especially value was getting to know Fred.

**Jenny:** How do we tell that story without making success sound like the proof that every decision was right?

**Wes:** 9. He took a special liking to us from the beginning and I believe it was genuine. He was generous with his time. If his time could be translated to dollars, I think it would have been measured in the hundreds of thousands if not millions of dollars worth of his time that he gave us.

**Wes:** 10. I learned a great deal about the process of funding Such a project, the language of finance, The process of building a presentation that qualified investors need to see.

**Wes:** 11. Perhaps the biggest lesson that I've learned is that regardless of what I envision, my plans cannot supersede the peace or lack of it that My plans cause Jenny and i as as a family

**Jenny:** Where did family peace belong in that decision?

**Wes:** 12. I don't know what I would have done differently other than pay attention to Jenny's resistance sooner.
