# Jenny: Coastal Surrender

**Narrator:** This chapter is written as a crafted Wes-and-Jenny dialogue from project-room source material. It is not a transcript. The conversation goal is to let Wes and Jenny examine the lesson carried by Jenny: Coastal Surrender.

**Jenny:** Several years after moving to North Carolina, and after buying the beach condo, I remarried. I genuinely believed this would be a forever relationship, but unfortunately, that was not the case, and the marriage ended rather abruptly almost before it began. I remember that it was amazingly great and it was amazingly horrible. At any rate, I came away from it with a real sense of raw emptiness, a huge sense of failure, very hard on myself for what seemed to be poor judgment and lack of my usual caution.

**Jenny:** You might even say I was a bit jaded against the institution, if not men in general. I wanted to run away, escape from the pain, the gossip, people who were reminders, and especially the threats I encountered, making me fearful for my safely. Where to go- My grown kids were away at college in three different states, none in NC. I definitely couldn’t go back to N. Va, much too expensive, not to mention how much I disliked the winters there.

**Wes:** What do you want the reader to understand about what that cost you emotionally?

**Jenny:** I was quite familiar with the area near my beach condo in SC, having vacationed there many times in younger years. so I decided I would move to that area, rent a place inland, and start over. It sounded perfect. I spent several days at my beach condo, searching for a place to live. It was winter, the off season, so no one was renting my condo. I was sure I would quickly find a place and everything would fall into place.

**Jenny:** Doors closed, not opened. I was rejected over and over for this reason or that reason, having a 70 pound Poodle was a frequent one. Three long days passed and I was empty handed. The last night before I would be heading back to Raleigh, I felt despair, like never before. Alone. Exhausted. Empty.

**Jenny:** As I sat down on the sofa, opened my laptop to search one more time, it slipped from my hands and landed on the tile floor and the entire screen turned to what reminded me of an Etch a Sketch drawing. At the end of my rope. Nothing left in me, and I cried out to the Lord to help me.

**Wes:** Where did your caution help you, and where did fear begin to take over?

**Jenny:** I’m ashamed to admit, I actually looked at the 15th floor balcony door, and considered….Just then, my phone rang, and the one and only person I knew who lived in the area was calling. At 10 pm. Linda. My angel. I tried to sound fine. I answered, and she immediately asked, “Are you OK-” I answered, I was, and she said, almost an order, “I’m coming over there.” 20 minutes, while I stared at my scrambled computer screen. I prayed. And prayed.

**Jenny:** She arrived, saw my laptop screen, called her husband, and together they restored it, somehow. She stayed for awhile and reassured me that things would turn around. The next morning, I packed, loaded my car and started driving away, toward Raleigh, having no idea what I was going to do or where I was going to go. As I approached the outskirts of the coastal area, my phone rang, and one of the management companies I had contacted and left messages was calling.

**Jenny:** They said, “We have several condos for you to choose from.” I turned the car around, and drove straight to their office in Pawley’s Island, took my Poodle inside with me, so they could see he was part of the package, if they were going to reject us. The very nice lady handed me 2 keys, gave me directions to the new condo development, and said go and choose!

**Wes:** How did waiting change what you thought God was doing?

**Jenny:** The condos were brand new, and beautiful, but could I afford the rent- I went back to the office, applied, and was accepted to move in immediately. The overwhelming relief and gratitude was beyond words. I surrendered my stronghold, my grip on the outcome, and the need to fix it myself. I was more dependent on the Lord than ever before in my life. He was there the whole time. I just had to stop and let go.

**Narrator:** This chapter deliberately leaves some tension unresolved. The source material does not require the reader to see every ending before recognizing that faith, patience, and caution are still doing their work.
