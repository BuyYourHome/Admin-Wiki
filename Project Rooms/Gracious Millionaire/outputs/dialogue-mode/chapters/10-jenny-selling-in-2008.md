# Jenny: Selling In 2008

**Narrator:** This chapter is written as a crafted Wes-and-Jenny dialogue from project-room source material. It is not a transcript. The conversation goal is to let Wes and Jenny examine the lesson carried by Jenny: Selling In 2008.

**Jenny:** The year was 2008. If you mention 2008 to any real estate investor today, they will most likely cringe at the memory. It was the year the real estate market collapsed, and triggered a subprime mortgage crisis. Home prices fell dramatically, and there I was caught in the cross hairs. It was a good time to buy, not a good time to sell, but I had no choice. I didn’t have the education that I have now. I didn’t have the experience that I have now.

**Jenny:** I certainly didn’t have the confidence I’ve grown into in recent years. And my condo sat on the market. No showings. No offers. And the clock was ticking financially. But I knew My Heavenly Father had me in His hand. I can’t say I wasn’t afraid, but I was clinging to Him and His Promises. He had a plan. I had to wait for Him to show me. That wasn’t easy, and I was anything but patient.

**Wes:** What do you want the reader to understand about what that cost you emotionally?

**Jenny:** Thinking back to the time after the condo remodeling, I had hired a lady who lived in the same building to clean before the realtor pictures and the showings that would follow. She knew many of the absentee owners and did cleaning for a number of them. She spoke to one of them about my condo being for sale, and just like that, I had THE BUYER!
