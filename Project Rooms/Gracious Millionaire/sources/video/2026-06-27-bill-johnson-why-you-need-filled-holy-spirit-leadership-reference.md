## Relevant Window

Primary window reviewed: approximately 47:00 through 60:00.

Key ideas for the manuscript:

- Authority is connected to commission and submission to the primary mission.
- The disciples had functioned under Jesus' authority and anointing, but after the resurrection they had to receive and carry their own authority and power for the mission.
- Working near someone else's gift can make a person seem to function in that gift, but leadership formation requires receiving and carrying what God has actually entrusted to that person.
- The Gideon image emphasizes yielding to the Holy Spirit rather than trying to force outcomes from personal strength.
- Acts frames Jesus as doing and teaching; leadership credibility is tied to lived practice, not concepts alone.
- The distinction is useful for Wes's leadership chapter: real leadership is not the pressure to make others adopt a plan. It is submitted authority, lived example, and allowing God to clothe the assignment with His power.
