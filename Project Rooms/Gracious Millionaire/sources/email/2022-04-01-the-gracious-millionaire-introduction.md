## Introduction Draft

In writing this book we are coming from the point of view of what we believe the Bible teaches concerning the Kingdom of God and how it relates to our finances. It is our belief that the Kingdom touches every aspect of life.

There are many books written about success and prosperity. There are many good books, books from all kinds of perspectives, that teach how to obtain success. You probably should read them. We have read some of them, enjoyed and learned from them. We do not claim to be experts at anything.

This is not a how-to book. Maybe it is a how-to-be book. This is our story of our adventures with real estate investment and the lessons we have or are learning.

Rudyard Kipling wrote a great poem entitled "If." You should read it, but there is this stanza that personifies a lesson that I am still learning.

If you can meet with triumph and disaster  
And treat those two impostors just the same;

...

Yours is the Earth and everything that's in it,  
And - which is more - you'll be a Man, my son!

Jenny and I have made a million dollars in a short period of time in real estate. The bulk of this book will be the stories of some of these real estate transactions. They are the life lessons that have been set before us to teach us about the Kingdom of God. We will be as honest as we can be about these stories and what we have learned.

At the time I am writing this I am building a framework for this book. The framework is a set of Biblical principles that I want to hang stories upon that we have experienced in real estate investment. I know, while I am writing this, that I do not have all of these stories identified. I do not have these lessons learned. But while I am writing this I am praying that God will give the examples needed to adequately reveal the truths outlined in the chapter headings below.

### Chapters

1. Favor - What is it?

Favor is something that I have been learning about deeply for about 8 years. I should credit the person who has been the most influential to us in this regard. I started watching YouTubes of Lance Wallnau about 8 years ago when I was in between jobs. Favor is something that you do not just have because of your position or experience. Education and expertise is not the key component of favor.

Remember everything we are saying here pivots upon our relationship to the King of this Kingdom.

Jesus grew in wisdom and in stature and in favor with God and all the people. Luke 2:52

I believe that from a Christian point of view, a Christian who is striving for the Kingdom of God, success, as you might define it, cannot and should not be the goal.

Let me back up and speak to the person who is not coming from this point of view. You can follow the principles of these books and have great success. However, if you are a Christian striving for the Kingdom of God, your success will be very limited if success, prosperity, wealth, fame, or influence is your goal.

Why is that? Because God is more interested in what is happening to you internally than what is happening to you externally.

But how can you write a book titled *The Gracious Millionaire* and not be teaching about success? Let me say that I do want to be successful. I even want to be wealthy. But when I put any of these goals ahead of God's purposes I have always found that God has another card to play.

Have a kind and generous spirit because gratitude is a secret to all magnificent success.

Grace and gratitude come from the same Latin word *gratia*, meaning favor, esteem, regard, a pleasing quality, goodwill, and from earlier roots meaning to announce, saying praise, celebrate. In English its first meaning was like divine favor, love, or assistance, typically unmerited assistance by the way, but freely given anyway.
