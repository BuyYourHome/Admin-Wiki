Providence Landing.

Without any doubt Providence Landing was the biggest project we have ever attempted. It is a long story with lots of lessons, some unfinished. To begin the story, I will have to make a confession. It is nothing I am proud of, but the story cannot be told without it.

Jenny and I were fighting about some aspects of the business. Our conversation had gotten heated, and I told her that I was going to get away for a few days because I needed a break from our relationship. I hurriedly packed a few things in a bag, grabbed my computer and headed out somewhere without a plan. I was clear headed enough to tell her I would be back but that was about all that I saw clearly.

Pulling out of our driveway and our neighborhood, I allowed many thoughts to pass through my brain about what I was going to do. One of the thoughts that gelled was that I had been communicating, via Facebook messenger, with a guy in Virginia that had about 80 acres for sale. I was frequently looking for acreage that we could buy the way that we buy houses. In other words, buy with terms that we could easily afford. What was I going to do with the property? I do not know, but one idea that I had been thinking about for several months was to build an airpark. I will explain what that means later.

I set my sights on Virginia and started trying to reach the property owner. Looking back at the text was some GPS coordinates. I will not bore you with the details of how I found the property, but that information was not sufficient. I did find the property and talked to the owner who gave me a tour of what he owned. It had been pouring rain the whole day since I left Raleigh. It had let up some as the owner drove me around his property. I was attracted to it and the price was not bad, but I concluded quickly that it was not going to serve the purpose of an airpark. I thanked him and left the area as the rain intensified.

Driving around, aimlessly, in some fierce storms, I decided to find a hotel in South Boston, Va. I got some groceries and went to the hotel to get out of my wet clothes, take a shower, and relax. My attitude was horrible, my disposition was depressed, as was my energy level. I had not treated my wife well and there was really nothing about my response that I was proud of.

I was just going through the motions of what I knew to do. I went to bed that way and woke up that way. After a morning shower and breakfast, I fell back on the bed and looked up at the ceiling, expecting to see God there. Going through the motions sometimes works. My lifetime habit is to look to God even when I am off track with his best expectations for my life. I looked at the ceiling and said out loud, **"God I do not know if you can speak to me but I am listening."** Hearing nothing, after a few minutes, I reengaged with my motions, those ones that I was going through. I walked over to my laptop and started searching for acreage in southern Virginia.
