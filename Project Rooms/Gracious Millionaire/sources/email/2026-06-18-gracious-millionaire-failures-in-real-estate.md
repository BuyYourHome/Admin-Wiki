I'm only going to refer to my second wife as number two in this narration.

Somewhere in the course of this period of time I got a hold of some of the real estate educational materials that I am still paying attention to. I began to think in terms of real estate along the lines of the mentors that I was listening to. It was the beginning of my real estate education.

I've been a Christian all my life. I've tried to walk in a way that was consistent with what I understood about the relationship I should have with God. There are deep-rooted beliefs that I have that started when I was just a child. I was brought up well by my parents in the understanding of God and what my relationship should look like. I honor the way my parents taught me my relationship with the Lord.

But there were aspects of my relationship with God that I have now that I did not understand then. I don't think I knew anything about walking in favor of the Lord. I think I knew a good bit about proper confession, but I don't think I was walking in it. All of that is just a little bit of context for some of the history I am going to relate now.

There were three major real estate transactions that my ex and I engaged in that in the end were total failures. I think if I knew what I know now they might not have been. However, that's something that we'll never know but a proper story about my life as a real estate investor would not be complete without talking about these failures.

The first transaction was a home for us to live in when we were in Raleigh. I would travel back and forth between Houston and Raleigh and pick up my kids and they would live with me while I was here.

The home we bought had two floors. It had a finished three bedroom, two bath first floor. The first floor opened up to the roadside and the driveway. It had an unfinished basement that was the same size as the upstairs and it opened up from the back of the property. This was my first major renovation. We designed and built four bedrooms and two bathrooms and a kitchen in that open unfinished basement. It was quite a project that I did almost entirely myself. I designed it and worked with a general contractor to do all of the work with a few exceptions. I didn't do the HVAC or the plumbing but I did everything else.

This design, once it was completed, allowed the family to move downstairs. The intention was to rent the upstairs to pay the mortgage on the property. This worked very well and was a very sound financial model. We effectively had no expense for our personal home in Raleigh.

The second major real estate transaction was the purchase of a large hotel in the Galveston area of Texas. Number two was from the Houston area and had a home there. I traveled back and forth between Houston and Raleigh during the period of our marriage. I shared custody of my four children with my first wife and that kept me with one foot on the ground in Raleigh. It was a very busy time of my life.

We bought a 200 room rundown hotel right off of a major highway going between Houston and Galveston. We bought it from a private owner. It was not operational when we bought it but it had a lot of potential. It had a restaurant, a bar, and three conference rooms. Many of the rooms needed a lot of maintenance to be able to be used. We structured a purchase agreement with the seller that involved some cash and a promissory note to the seller for the remainder of the purchase.

I don't have any of the records from this acquisition. Those were all maintained by my ex and the purchase was in some entities that we set up that she had control of. So my memory is a little fuzzy after all these years and I'll only talk in general terms about the money. I think the total purchase price was around $3 million.

When we bought it we moved into one of the suites and began to live and breathe the operation of the hotel. Everything had to be brought up to operational condition. We hired consultants to advise us on operation of the restaurant and conference rooms. We catered food for the events that we were attracting for the conference facilities. We got a set of the rooms operational and began to have guests in the hotel.

It was an exciting time of my life. I'm not trying to give a lesson on operating a hotel. I have experience but nothing to teach. I'm just giving some context and backstory to the story Jenny and I are relating.

The hotel never made money and as the financial pressures built so did the stress in our marriage. I made the decision in conjunction with my ex-wife to move back permanently to Raleigh where I could get a job to begin to stem the losses that we had endured. We eventually lost the investment.

The third major investment that we made was five duplexes in Johnston County, North Carolina. They were purchased from one individual and were all occupied with what was called Section 8 housing residents at the time.

They were bought around the same time as the hotel acquisition and I was going to manage them during my trips back and forth to Raleigh. I think I got quite an education into property management at that time. I learned a lot but again in the end they were lessons from failures.

As my marriage with number two began to fall apart amidst the stress of the finances from all the investments, I made a deal with her to buy her out of the duplexes. The loans had been acquired in her name. So I created a partnership agreement and entity with my mom to pay back the investment that my ex had made and we transferred the deeds into our name.

The divorce was initiated and she demanded that we sell the house. I offered to buy it from her and had a loan in place to do so but she would have nothing to do with it and demanded that it be sold. She sold it and took the proceeds as she had invested the money.

I moved into a nearby apartment where I would be able to house my children. We stayed there for a little over a year until my children were old enough to move out except for my youngest.

I bought an RV that was big enough for me to live in permanently and to have room for my youngest son to stay when he was with me. We parked the RV in the backyard of one of the duplexes where I could keep a close eye on the tenants and effectively manage the properties.

We kept the five loans in place and took ownership subject to the existing mortgages. For a while they were cash flowing well enough to pay all the expenses. Little by little I had trouble keeping them rented at the level needed to pay the mortgages and they all started getting behind.

This was at the time of the 2008 mortgage crisis. The bank was not very tolerant of the delinquencies. Honestly, I didn't have the experience or education to find ways to avoid the consequences. One by one the banks foreclosed on all of them.

Over a period of five years we had bought a hotel, a personal residence, five rental duplexes, and some other investment properties. At the end of that time we had lost all of it and our marriage had ended in failure.

I just thought it was important to present this period of failure in context to what we are presenting now as great success. The only great difference I can recognize is that now we are walking in the favor of the Lord and that then we were not.
