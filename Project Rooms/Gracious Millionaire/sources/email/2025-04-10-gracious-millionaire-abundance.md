Isaiah 59:19-20 New King James Version (NKJV)

So shall they fear The name of the LORD from the west, And His glory from the rising of the sun; When the enemy comes in like a flood, The Spirit of the LORD will lift up a standard against him.

What God is wanting from us is lordship. He provides abundance but it looks different for every person. Jesus said the kingdom of God is within you. So all kingdom issues are heart issues. And all heart issues have to do with lordship.

There are three stories Jesus provides that show lordship does not look the same for each of us. He does not demand the same circumstances from all of us, except that he is our Lord.

1. The rich young ruler was missing one thing. He was told to sell everything he had and give to the poor.
2. Zacchaeus, walking with Jesus, having a conversation, said he would give half.
3. Peter said he had left all to follow Jesus. Jesus' response was Mark 10: you shall receive a hundred times as much, in this life, for what you left, with persecution.

We must be faithful in little to be trusted with much. The more breakthrough we get, the more opposition we get.

The story of the landowner paying wages pays the same to him who worked one hour as the one who worked 12 hours, but all for what they agreed to. Shine for justice. Do not for extreme grace.

We have generosity, contentment, and excellence with what we have. We have to know how to handle what we have been provided.
