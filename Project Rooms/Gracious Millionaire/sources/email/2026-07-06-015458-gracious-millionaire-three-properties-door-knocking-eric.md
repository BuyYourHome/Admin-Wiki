1. There are three properties that I'd like to talk about all together.

2. I collected a few properties from our our foreclosure list together to go out and do what we called Door knocking. These would be properties that looked particularly interesting in on paper. I would go visit the properties one by one. See what they look like, see if anybody was currently living in the houses and just look at the neighborhood. Try to make a assessment of whether we would be interested in them or not.

3. This day I had three properties in mind and I went to see all of them. They were all vacant estates. They were all in need of significant repair and I was interested in all of them.

4. I came back and told Jenny that I thought we could buy all of them. And to jump to the end of the story we did.

5. So these are properties that we would have already been mailing to. Seems to me like we'd already gotten return mail on all of them so I had a pretty good idea. They were baking before I left

6. Getting returned mail from your mailers might go directly into most investors trash can. But we needed these owners were going to be harder to find and so most investors would not give it any more effort than that. But we know that if we can find these oners, they are more incentivize, more motivated to sell us their properties for a very reasonable price.

7. There was going to be a lot of work in store for me to find the errors of these properties but eventually I did

8. Eric was the most intriguing of the three. It had a nice fenced in yard in a rural area of Johnson county.

9. The inside was accessible through the back door which was unlocked and the property had clearly been ransacked. Everything had been gone through but no effort to clean out. The property had been done so it was full of old furniture, old clothes, kitchenware, etc. But clearly everything had been gone through was scattered to the floors

10. This property from our research had a lot of siblings as heirs. Most investors would stop and for good reason but I continue to pursue it

11. I only found one of the Earth to be local and I went by the address and knocked on the door and answered. She told me the property belonged to her husband's family and agreed to take my phone number and give it to her husband

12. He agreed to meet me and I came by his house and spoke to him briefly where he informed me that he would be willing to sell the house to me but that I was never going to be able to buy it because his half-siblings would have nothing to do with it

13. He said it would give me contact information for all of them if I made it worth his while. So I gave him a contract agreeing to pay each of The five of them $3,000 a piece. I signed the contract and gave him $100 due diligence and he gave me all of their information.

14. It turned out that there was another half brother who lived in the area and after having contact him I met him and explained the deal to him also.

15. With some reluctance he also agreed to the deal and said he would talk to his three sisters that lived in other states

16. Eventually all of them agreed to the contract, signed it in front of a notary and I wired them each their money and we own the house.

17. There was a big scare that came along with this property eventually and taught us to be more diligent in our research of properties. After we had the contracts signed and I paid the owners, the heirs their money and reinstated the mortgage that was headed towards foreclosure. My attorney found an IRS lien on the property for something like $60,000. That scared Jenny and I and what was looking like a very profitable deal was suddenly a lot skinnier. However, there was still enough profit in it to continue to move forward on it. Plus, we've already committed to hit with our finances

18. We proceeded with are remodeling of the property. Actually we were cutting corners where we could to try to save the budget. That wasn't nearly as healthy as we thought it was.

19. However, after researching it trying to figure out how I had missed it. I recognized that the person who had the IRS lien shared  a name with the previous honor but not a social security number. So the lean wasn't actually on this property and we were in good shape.

20. There's a couple of clear lessons in this property. The first is do your due diligence. The second is don't be so quick to give up on a prospect. To some extent, these two lessons are intention with one another. Move forward as quickly as you can. Work hard to get a deal but slow down and do your due diligence. All of that working against each other can be discouraging. But I found that if you don't let discouragement deter you and you keep putting one foot in front of another plans often unfold that you could not have comprehended. That is especially true when you are relying on the favor of God. Sometimes what looks like reckless risk-taking to others just works out for those who have favor.
