My First Experiences with Buying Real Estate

Wes has mentioned failed marriages.  I never thought I would be divorced, but
here I am with two failed marriages. At 23 years old, I married “Number One
Husband”. Both of us wanted to own our own home, so that first year of
marriage, while living in an apartment, we worked two jobs each and saved
until we had enough money for a down payment.  We bought a small starter home
the CONVENTIONAL way of borrowing from a bank, with a 30 year mortgage, that
we could afford with both of us working full time. It cost $37,000, and the
year was 1978 in Northern Virginia.  That was a lot of money to me, but I was
confident that we could afford it. Fast forward to 1980, we wanted to start a
family. Neither of us had fond memories of going to the babysitter while our
parents worked, so we vowed I would be a stay at home mom, somehow.  Number
One was working for his brother, but wanted to start his own remodeling
business, so we sold my beautiful 260Z sports car to buy him a truck. I loved
that car, but my priorities were changing as I looked forward to having a
baby, and staying home to raise children. As is my ‘safe’  nature, we saved
every paycheck I got for one solid year to see if we could afford for me to
quit working. I can see now how God honored that commitment, although at the
time, I may have thought it was our good planning. In 1981 our first child, a
son, was born, and I quit my full time job (that I didn’t like anyway) and
never really went back to being an employee again, at least not in the years
of raising children. Twenty-three months later, we were blessed with twin
girls, who were quite a surprise! Number One said I wanted, and prayed so hard
for A GIRL, that the Lord gave me TWO! Haha!  It was a very chaotic time, with
3 babies under the age of 2, and overnight we outgrew not only our starter
home, but also our Cutlass Sedan.  Three car seats could not be wedged into
the back seat of our “family car”.  We bought a larger, newly built house for
$99,000 that could easily accommodate  3 cribs, and a used Dodge Ram Van with
seating for 8!  (Not that I wanted to fill all those seats, rest assured I
wanted no more babies!) That was a time when banks were charging big interest
rates, and I remember at one time we had a 13 ¾% rate.   My days were filled
with changing diapers and nursing crying babies, and I had little time, or
energy, for anything or anyone else. Looking back, I speculate it was a tough
time for Number One,  no doubt feeling somewhat neglected, and getting saddled
with large expenses, such as a big monthly mortgage, and a sizable used car
loan to pay. We could barely make ends meet, and it created a lot of stress in
the marriage. Soon enough, we were getting behind in bills, then catching up,
then repeating the cycle again.  Number One coped by not working full time,
and spending money that we didn’t have to spend. This pattern would eventually
cause the marriage to end, after 25 years together. Fortunately, the kids were
out of the house and away at college. We sold that house for several hundred
thousand dollars over what we owed, and split the pay out and went our
separate ways.



I moved to Raleigh, NC in 2002, and took the proceeds from the home sale and
invested much of it into buying an ocean front condo in Garden City Beach, SC,
south of Myrtle Beach. I carefully calculated how the condo would rent out to
vacationers, how much the HOA fees were, insurance, etc., and hired an onsite
management company to take care of the rest. They charged me 40%! Rest
assured, I had done my homework, and 40% was the going rate up and down the
beach. I had no intention of ever living there, it was only an investment.
That first summer, the rentals paid for an entire year of mortgage payments,
and I thought I was on my way.  In all my careful research and planning, I had
failed to investigate how/if the HOA dues, and assessments might increase, and
within 3-4 years, those fees had put me in the red, even though the rental
monies easily paid each and every one of my mortgage payments. I guess you
could say I panicked. This was all on me, and I didn’t see a way to stay
afloat. I got a contractor to completely remodel the condo, and I placed it on
the market for sale.  The year was 2008.  If you mention 2008 to any real
estate investor today, they will most likely cringe at the memory. It was the
year the real estate market collapsed, and triggered a subprime mortgage
crisis. Home prices fell dramatically, and there I was caught in the cross
hairs. It was a good time to buy, not a good time to sell, but I had no
choice. I didn’t have the education that I have now.  I didn’t have the
experience that I have now. I certainly didn’t have the confidence I’ve grown
into in recent years. And my condo sat on the market.  No showings. No offers.
And the clock was ticking financially. But I knew My Heavenly Father had me in
His hand. I can’t say I wasn’t afraid, but I was clinging to Him and His
Promises. He had a plan.  I had to wait for Him to show me.  That wasn’t easy,
and I was anything but patient. Thinking back to the time after the condo
remodeling, I had hired a lady who lived in the same building to clean before
the realtor pictures and the showings that would follow. She knew many of the
absentee owners and did cleaning for a number of them.  She spoke to one of
them about my condo being for sale, and just like that, I had THE BUYER!
