# Interview v1 Jean Question Map

Date: 2026-07-09

Purpose: Record the function of Jean's questions in Interview mode so the manuscript reads like a guided interview without inventing unsupported memories.

| Chapter range | Jean's question function | Repetition-control rule |
|---|---|---|
| Foreword and opening | Define the format, identify the governing question, and keep money under lordship. | Ask the money question fully once, then return to it only when the chapter requires a new angle. |
| Jenny chapters | Invite Jenny's own sequence and safety-oriented interpretation before Wes comments elsewhere. | Do not interrupt Jenny's story with Wes's theology or business interpretation. |
| Failure, presence, and convergence | Ask how pressure formed faith before later success arrives. | Avoid repeating "God was there" as a slogan; require a concrete pressure point in each answer. |
| Early-property and private-money chapters | Ask what each deal taught about diligence, order, trust, and responsibility. | Do not re-explain every property as the same lesson. Each chapter gets one primary formation question. |
| Leadership and favor chapters | Ask where vision becomes control and where favor becomes assignment. | Do not let favor language replace obedience, patience, or accountability. |
| Providence Landing chapters | Ask discovery, review, and letting-go questions in sequence. | Keep Providence Landing to three distinct movements: imagination, business review, release. |
| Rosebrooks and Orphaned Property | Ask how accusation, uncertainty, and spousal caution test the heart. | Do not resolve unresolved outcomes just to make the narrative feel complete. |
| Abundance, gratitude, tribes, and closing | Ask what increase is for, what complaint reveals, and how shared work should be formed. | End with unfinished learning rather than repeating a victory lap. |

Jean may draw on contextual source material for question quality, but Jean must not become the authority over Wes's or Jenny's private convictions.
