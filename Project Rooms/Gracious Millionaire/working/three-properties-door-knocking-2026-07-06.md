## Eric Source Points

- Wes had three foreclosure-list properties in mind and visited all three on the same door-knocking trip.
- All three appeared to be vacant estates needing significant repair.
- Wes came back and told Jenny he thought they could buy all three, and ultimately they did.
- Returned mail was not treated as a dead end. It signaled that the owners would be harder to find, which meant many investors would stop.
- Eric was the most intriguing of the three: a rural Johnston County property with a fenced yard.
- The back door was unlocked, the house had been ransacked, and belongings were scattered through the house.
- Research showed multiple sibling heirs.
- One local contact said the property belonged to her husband's family and passed Wes's number along.
- The local heir was willing to sell but warned that his half-siblings would not cooperate.
- Wes offered five heirs $3,000 each, paid $100 due diligence, and obtained contact information.
- Wes later discovered another local half-brother, explained the deal, and eventually all heirs agreed, signed before notaries, and were wired their money.
- After the contracts were signed, money paid, and the mortgage reinstated, the attorney found an apparent IRS lien of about $60,000.
- The scare taught Wes and Jenny to be more diligent in property research.
- Further research showed the IRS lien belonged to someone with the same name as the prior owner but a different Social Security number, so the lien was not actually attached to the property.
