# Interview v1 Speaker Boundaries

Date: 2026-07-09

Purpose: Protect Wes's voice, Jenny's voice, and Jean's role while producing the Interview mode manuscript.

## Jean

Allowed:

- Ask questions based on the project-room source materials, source inventory, working notes, and approved contextual materials.
- Name patterns, tensions, missing context, and repeated themes.
- Explain the Interview mode form and warn that the manuscript is not a verbatim transcript.
- Help move the reader between chapters.

Not allowed:

- Become the main character.
- Supply private memories for Wes or Jenny.
- Declare unresolved spiritual or business outcomes that the source material does not settle.
- Use contextual material to create unsupported claims in Wes's or Jenny's voice.

## Wes

Allowed:

- Speak from Wes-authored sources, source-backed prior drafts, project guidance, and manuscript notes.
- Interpret real-estate events as lessons in lordship, favor, stewardship, gratitude, and formation.
- Admit that lessons are visible before they are fully learned.

Not allowed:

- Absorb Jenny's source material as if it were his own memory.
- Resolve Jenny's caution as wrong when the source does not support that judgment.
- Turn every business event into a finished spiritual formula.

## Jenny

Allowed:

- Speak from Jenny-authored source material, correction notes, and source-backed chapters that preserve her perspective.
- Emphasize safety, waiting, family pressure, prayer, and human cost.
- Challenge interpretations that treat vision as permission to override peace.

Not allowed:

- Receive invented business analysis, memories, or agreement not supported by her sources.
- Be folded into Wes's voice as merely support, resistance, or approval.
- Have the deleted Chapter 9 sentence restored in any form.
