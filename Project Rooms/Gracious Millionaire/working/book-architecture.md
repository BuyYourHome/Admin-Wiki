# Gracious Millionaire Book Architecture

## Purpose

The Book Architecture is the shared, source-derived editorial map for every Gracious Millionaire manuscript mode. It separates whole-book organization from mode-specific prose so that Quick, Rewrite, Dialogue, and Interview modes can benefit from the same structural reasoning without using another mode's manuscript as source material.

Rewrite mode is the primary architecture-discovery process. It discovers or refreshes the architecture from original project-room sources before drafting its own manuscript.

## Authority And Independence

- Original source files, current factual and correction notes, approved contextual sources, book objectives, and current Wes/Jenny direction control the architecture.
- The Book Architecture is a working editorial map, not a manuscript and not polished prose.
- Rewrite mode may discover structural relationships, but Rewrite manuscript prose does not become part of the architecture.
- No manuscript mode output may be used as source material for the architecture or for another mode.
- Every architecture assignment must be traceable to original source material or clearly marked as an editorial proposal, unresolved question, or missing-context need.
- All modes may use the current approved Book Architecture for sequence, coverage, chapter purpose, thematic relationships, voice assignment, and structural decisions.

## Required Architecture Content

Maintain the following information in this file or in linked, versioned architecture working notes:

- Current parts, chapters, sections, and sequence.
- Stable story-unit identifiers for events, properties, lessons, themes, and unresolved questions.
- Original source references supporting each story unit.
- Chronology and property sequence where relevant.
- The current chapter assignment for each story unit.
- Wes, Jenny, Shared, or Alternating voice assignment for each chapter or story unit.
- The purpose or job of each chapter.
- Themes carried, complicated, or resolved by each chapter.
- Relationships and dependencies between story units and chapters.
- Recommended chapter moves, splits, joins, removals, and transitions.
- Duplicate, conflicting, unsupported, and still-unused material.
- Open factual, emotional, theological, or editorial questions that should remain unresolved until supported.

Use stable story-unit identifiers so material can move between chapters without losing its source trail.

## Rewrite Mode Responsibility

At the beginning of a requested Rewrite mode run:

1. Rebuild or refresh the Book Architecture from the complete current original-source set.
2. Reconsider the whole book in light of the latest sources rather than preserving the prior chapter arrangement by default.
3. Record source-supported chapter moves, splits, joins, sequence changes, voice assignments, and thematic relationships in the architecture.
4. Distinguish source-supported structural conclusions from editorial proposals and unresolved major-direction decisions.
5. Draft the Rewrite manuscript independently from original sources under the refreshed architecture.

A Rewrite manuscript is one product of the architecture. It is not the architecture itself.

## Quick Mode Responsibility

When Quick mode processes new or corrected source material:

1. Update the affected story units and source references in the Book Architecture.
2. Determine whether the source requires a Simple Quick update or a Structural Quick update.
3. For a Simple Quick update, revise the affected passage or chapter while preserving the current structure.
4. For a Structural Quick update, apply the architecture's required moves, splits, joins, resequencing, voice changes, and transitions across the affected chapter group.
5. Preserve chapters outside the affected structural group unless a factual correction or dependency requires a scoped change.
6. Draft all changed Quick-mode prose from original sources and the current Quick manuscript state, not from Rewrite or another mode's prose.

Quick mode is not required to reconsider the entire manuscript on every intake. It is required to keep its affected chapters aligned with the current Book Architecture.

## Other Modes

- Dialogue mode uses the architecture to determine chapter sequence, story coverage, speaker ownership, themes, and unresolved questions, then independently creates dialogue from original sources.
- Interview mode uses the architecture to determine thematic progression, question opportunities, speaker ownership, and unresolved questions, then independently creates interview prose under its existing source and Quick-reference rules.
- A mode may vary presentation and prose form, but it should document any intentional departure from the current architecture.

## Architecture Change Gate

- Routine, source-supported placement and structural changes may be adopted during an authorized manuscript-mode run.
- Major book-direction decisions that materially change the book's thesis, omit a substantial source-backed story, or reverse prior Wes/Jenny direction must be recorded as proposed and presented to Wes before being treated as approved architecture.
- Architecture changes must be recorded before the related manuscript is treated as review-ready.
- The manuscript quality review must confirm that chapter order, story coverage, voice ownership, and transitions match the current architecture or identify intentional exceptions.
