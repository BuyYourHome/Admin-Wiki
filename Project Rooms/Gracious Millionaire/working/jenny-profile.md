## What The Current Manuscript Shows

Jenny is presented as Wes's wife, business partner, and spiritual companion in the real estate story behind *Gracious Millionaire*. The manuscript frames the book as "our story," not only Wes's story, even though most of the existing source text is in Wes's first-person voice.

Jenny appears in the current source material as someone whose presence changes the meaning of the business. The real estate work is not merely a set of deals Wes is doing alone. It is a shared life, shared pressure, shared prayer, and shared formation.

## Likely Role In The Book

Jenny's role should not be reduced to agreement with Wes. The sources show her as a meaningful counterweight, witness, and discernment partner.

In the 115 Rosebrooks chapter, Jenny's hurt matters because she knew the groceries, rides, visits, and care that were part of the relationship with Brynda. That means Jenny's perspective carries the relational evidence of the story. She remembers the care, not merely the paperwork.

In the Tensity/Rose Place chapter, Jenny is not automatically enthusiastic about every creative opportunity. She does not like the idea of acquiring lower-end properties just because the numbers might work. That caution is important. It represents a stewardship instinct that asks whether a profitable idea is also wise, manageable, and peaceful.

## Spiritual Posture

Jenny is part of the daily prayer rhythm in the manuscript. Wes describes the two of them meeting on the couch first thing every morning for devotions and prayer, praying about everything they are involved in or concerned with, and considering what God wants them to pay attention to.

That makes Jenny's voice important for the book's theme of lordship. The book should not present Jenny as a passive audience for Wes's discernment. It should present her as part of the shared process of hearing, waiting, disagreeing honestly, and seeking unity.

## Business Posture

Based on the current manuscript, Jenny appears to bring practical caution to the business. She is not against profit, but she is not drawn to every out-of-the-box structure merely because the upside is high.

When Wes is attracted to creative deal structures, unusual assets, and higher upside, Jenny may be more attentive to the burden, risk, and management reality of the opportunity. This difference should be treated respectfully in the manuscript. It is not a weakness in either of them. It is part of the way the marriage and business are being formed.

## Emotional And Relational Notes

Jenny's perspective may naturally emphasize:

- The human cost of business pressure.
- The relational side of helping people.
- The pain of being misunderstood when help is later accused or resisted.
- The importance of peace and unity in decisions.
- The practical weight of managing what looks profitable on paper.
- The need for prayer before action, especially when Wes is energized by a possibility.

## Drafting Guidance For Jenny's Submissions

When Jenny sends material for *Gracious Millionaire*:

- Preserve her actual perspective, even where it differs from Wes's.
- Do not force her voice to sound like Wes's voice.
- Let her be direct, practical, relational, and discerning.
- Watch for emotional truth, not only transaction facts.
- Keep her role active: wife, partner, witness, intercessor, and decision-maker.
- If she expresses caution, do not soften it into mere hesitation. Treat it as discernment unless her words show otherwise.
- If she describes hurt, frustration, or concern, preserve the weight of it without making the tone bitter.
- When Wes and Jenny differ, frame the difference as part of the book's lordship theme: unity has to be formed, not assumed.

## Things To Avoid

- Do not portray Jenny as merely supportive background.
- Do not make her sound like Wes with a different name.
- Do not turn caution into negativity.
- Do not turn disagreement into disunity.
- Do not invent her private thoughts or motives.
- Do not use this profile to override Jenny's later corrections.
