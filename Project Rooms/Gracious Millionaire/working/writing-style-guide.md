# Gracious Millionaire Writing Style Guide

## Purpose

Use this guide to reproduce Wes's writing voice for *Gracious Millionaire* while drafting from the email sources.

## Core Voice

The voice is first-person, reflective, candid, and faith-centered. It reads like a personal testimony shaped into a business memoir. The narrator is not trying to sound like an expert standing above the reader. He is working through lessons in public, using real estate stories as evidence of spiritual principles.

The dominant posture is humility: "I am learning this," not "I have mastered this." Success is acknowledged directly, but it is repeatedly subordinated to lordship, favor, gratitude, stewardship, and divine assignment.

## Sentence Rhythm

Use mostly plain, direct sentences. Let paragraphs move by thought association: a story detail leads to a spiritual observation, which leads to a question, which leads back to a business example.

The writing often uses short thesis sentences after a longer story setup:

- "This was not the end of this story."
- "Complaining distorts your view of reality."
- "Some things have to be said."
- "This is not a how-to book. Maybe it is a how-to-be book."

Preserve that rhythm. Do not over-polish every sentence into formal nonfiction. The style should feel spoken, prayerful, and self-examining.

## Narrative Pattern

Most chapter sections should follow this pattern:

1. Name the spiritual principle.
2. Admit the human weakness, confusion, or wrong motive.
3. Tell the real estate story in practical detail.
4. Notice where favor, assignment, gratitude, or lordship showed up.
5. Draw a lesson without pretending the lesson is fully mastered.

The story details matter. Include dates, places, phone calls, dollar amounts, spreadsheet conclusions, family conversations, and awkward moments. The concrete facts keep the spiritual claims grounded.

## Theological Frame

The writing assumes a Kingdom-of-God worldview. Money, real estate, favor, education, and opportunity are interpreted through lordship and assignment.

Key ideas to preserve:

- Favor is connected to divine assignment, not merely personal charm or skill.
- Prosperity is not the highest goal.
- God cares more about internal formation than external success.
- Gratitude, stewardship, and generosity are measurable in natural-world behavior.
- Complaining is treated as evidence of unbelief or misplaced lordship.
- Real estate work can be an arena for ministry, obedience, and provision.

Use scripture naturally, as part of the thinking process. Avoid making the prose sound like a sermon outline unless the source itself is clearly outline notes.

## Relationship To Reader

Speak to the reader as someone invited into a story, not as someone being lectured. The reader may or may not fully share the author's assumptions, so occasionally slow down and explain the point of view:

- "Let me back up and speak to the person who is not coming from this point of view."
- "Of course there is nothing specific about real estate in that definition."
- "But how can you write a book titled *The Gracious Millionaire* and not be teaching about success?"

Questions are useful. They let the author wrestle with tension instead of flattening it.

## Business And Real Estate Detail

Keep the business language practical and specific. The voice is comfortable discussing:

- Foreclosure files
- HOA foreclosures
- Subject-to structures
- Private money loans
- Seller conversations
- Equity and cash offers
- Spreadsheet profit projections
- Tenant-occupied property

Do not make the business sections slick or salesy. The real estate analysis should feel like Wes thinking through an opportunity and then recognizing a spiritual lesson inside it.

## Editing Rules For Reproduction

- Preserve first-person singular and plural: "I," "Jenny and I," and "we."
- Preserve humility and uncertainty where present.
- Keep rhetorical questions.
- Keep plain transitions such as "Let me back up," "Here is another example," and "That is the flavor of that appointment."
- Smooth obvious transcription errors only when they distract from meaning.
- Do not replace the author's directness with corporate polish.
- Do not turn every spiritual insight into a tidy maxim. Let some thoughts remain exploratory.

## Things To Avoid

- Do not write like a prosperity manual.
- Do not make the narrator sound triumphalist.
- Do not remove confession, weakness, or relational tension.
- Do not over-explain every Bible reference.
- Do not turn the real estate stories into generic case studies.
- Do not use a motivational-speaker tone unless the source clearly calls for it.
