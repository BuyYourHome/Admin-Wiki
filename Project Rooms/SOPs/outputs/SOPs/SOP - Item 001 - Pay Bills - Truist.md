# SOP - Item 001: Pay Bills - QuickBooks and Truist

## Purpose

Pay company bills through the correct system and record the payment so accounting stays current.

## When To Use

Use QuickBooks for non-recurring bills, variable monthly bills, and all utilities. Use Truist for recurring bills with the same monthly amount.

## Owner

- Role: Accounting / Office Assistant
- Backup: Wes or Jenny

## Category

Accounting

## Inputs Needed

- Bill or receipt to be paid
- Vendor information
- Correct company account
- Property address or class, when applicable
- Payment account

## Tools / Systems

- QuickBooks
- Truist online banking
- Vendor records

## Steps

1. Decide whether the bill should be paid through QuickBooks or Truist.
2. Use QuickBooks when the bill is non-recurring, changes amount monthly, or is a utility bill.
3. Use Truist when the bill is recurring and the monthly amount is fixed.
4. For QuickBooks payments, log in and choose the correct company account.
5. Select the vendor, or create the vendor if it does not already exist.
6. Open vendor details, add or correct vendor information, and save.
7. Create a new transaction.
8. Enter the payment amount and correct class, such as a property or company class.
9. Save the transaction and exit the transaction entry view.
10. Open the transaction list.
11. Choose Schedule & Pay.
12. Select the bank account.
13. Continue through the payment flow and select paper check when needed.
14. Submit the payment and confirm the date.
15. In the memo field, enter the property address when the bill relates to a property.
16. Confirm and schedule the payment.
17. Mark the receipt paid, including payment date and account paid from.
18. For Truist payments, switch to the correct company/account from the gear menu before paying.
19. Save or file confirmation according to the accounting filing process.

## Decision Rules

- Utilities go through QuickBooks even if they recur.
- Recurring same-amount bills can go through Truist.
- Property-related payments need the property address in the memo or class.

## Common Mistakes

- Paying from the wrong company account.
- Forgetting to mark the receipt paid after scheduling payment.
- Missing the property class or memo.
