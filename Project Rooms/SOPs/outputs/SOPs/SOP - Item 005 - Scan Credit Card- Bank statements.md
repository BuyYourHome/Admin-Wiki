# SOP - Item 005: Scan Credit Card and Bank Statements

## Purpose

Scan credit card and bank statements, save them in the correct Teams folders, and file the paper statements.

## When To Use

Use this when monthly credit card or bank statements arrive and need to be stored electronically and physically.

## Owner

- Role: Accounting / Office Assistant
- Backup: Jenny

## Category

Accounting

## Inputs Needed

- Paper credit card or bank statement
- Access to scanner
- Access to Office 365 and Teams
- Correct year and account folder

## Tools / Systems

- Office 365 Scan
- Microsoft Teams
- Credit card statement notebook
- Bank statement notebook

## Steps

1. Open Office 365 and choose Scan.
2. Open Microsoft Teams.
3. Go to Office Admin.
4. Open Files.
5. Open the appropriate year folder.
6. Open either Credit Cards or Bank Statements.
7. Open the folder for the correct account.
8. Scan every page of the statement, using Add for pages after page 1.
9. Save the scan.
10. Name the file with the statement closing date in year-month-day format, such as `22-06-16`.
11. If Teams asks whether to replace another file, confirm only if it is the correct duplicate or corrected scan.
12. Save the PDF into the correct account folder.
13. If scanning additional statements, return to the scan preview and repeat the process for the next account.
14. Put a check mark on the paper statement after it is scanned.
15. Three-hole punch the statement.
16. File credit card statements in the credit card notebook by account number.
17. File bank statements in the bank statement notebook.

## Decision Rules

- Use Credit Cards for credit card statements.
- Use Bank Statements for bank account statements.
- File by account, not by the person who scanned it.

## Common Mistakes

- Saving to the wrong year or account folder.
- Missing pages in a multi-page statement.
- Naming the file with the wrong date format.
