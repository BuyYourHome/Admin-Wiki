1. Build a Rose transaction fact sheet in `working`.
   - Purpose: lock parties, property, price, note terms, dates, and disclosures before document generation.

2. Draft the Contract for Deed Agreement first.
   - It controls the transaction and drives the memorandum, note, amortization schedule, and any security instrument.

3. Draft the Memorandum of Contract for Deed second.
   - It is shorter and recordable, and it should mirror the contract once core facts are set.

4. Draft the Promissory Note third.
   - It depends on finalized principal, interest, term, payment dates, balloon/prepayment terms, and default language.

5. Draft the Deed of Trust fourth, if confirmed needed.
   - It depends on the final note and correct grantor/trustee/beneficiary structure.

6. Prepare the amortization schedule after the payment terms are confirmed.
   - Use the Rose spreadsheet amortization tab as the source, then export a clean buyer-facing schedule.
