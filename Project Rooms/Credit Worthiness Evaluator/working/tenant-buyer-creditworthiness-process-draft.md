# Tenant-Buyer Creditworthiness Process Draft

## Status

Working draft for attorney and management review. Do not use as a final SOP yet.

## Purpose

Evaluate whether a tenant-buyer appears able to repay a proposed Contract for Deed obligation and preserve a written record showing that Buy Your Home considered consumer-credit compliance before approving the sale.

## Guardrails

- Do not approve a Contract for Deed based only on a tenant's desire to buy the home.
- Do not treat "one sale per year" as automatic Dodd-Frank compliance.
- Do not use negative amortization.
- Do not use adjustable-rate terms, balloon terms, prepayment penalties, or unusual default terms without attorney review.
- Do not rely on unverified income, debts, or payment claims when those facts are material to approval.
- Do not make exceptions based on protected characteristics or informal impressions.

## Step 1 - Transaction Screening

Before collecting detailed buyer financial records, create a transaction screen with:

- Seller legal name and entity type.
- Property address, state, county, and occupancy status.
- Confirmation whether buyer will use the property as a dwelling or principal dwelling.
- Number of seller-financed properties by the same seller in the last 12 months.
- Confirmation whether the seller constructed the home.
- Proposed purchase price, down payment, financed amount, interest rate, term, monthly payment, and final payment.
- Tax, insurance, HOA, repair, utility, and maintenance responsibilities.
- Any balloon payment, adjustable rate, prepayment penalty, late charge, default charge, or other nonstandard term.

Management must mark the file "Attorney review needed" if the seller is not clearly a natural person, estate, or trust, if the transaction includes a balloon or adjustable-rate feature, or if state law requirements have not been confirmed.

## Step 2 - Application Package

Collect the same categories from every applicant:

- Signed buyer application and authorization to verify records.
- Government ID.
- Current household income sources.
- Employment status if employment income is relied on.
- Recent pay stubs or payroll records.
- Recent bank statements.
- Most recent tax return or transcript if needed for self-employment or irregular income.
- Current lease ledger and rental-payment history.
- Credit report or other reliable credit-history record.
- List of current debts, alimony, child support, and recurring non-debt obligations.
- Evidence of assets or reserves if relied on for approval.

## Step 3 - Payment And Obligation Calculation

Calculate the buyer's full monthly housing burden:

- Contract principal and interest payment.
- Property taxes.
- Homeowners insurance.
- HOA or association charges.
- Required utilities if unusually high or property-specific.
- Repairs, maintenance, and buyer-paid property upkeep.
- Any simultaneous loan payment.
- Any required escrow or reserve payment.

Document the source for each amount. Use tax records, insurance quotes, HOA statements, contracts, or other reliable records where possible.

## Step 4 - Ability-To-Repay Review

Review and document:

- Current or reasonably expected income or assets relied on.
- Employment status if employment income is used.
- Monthly payment on the Contract for Deed.
- Monthly payment on any simultaneous loan.
- Mortgage-related obligations such as taxes, insurance, HOA dues, and required property charges.
- Current debts, alimony, and child support.
- Debt-to-income ratio or residual income.
- Credit history, including payment history, judgments, collections, bankruptcies, and rental history.

Use third-party records for material facts. If a fact cannot be verified, either exclude it from the approval basis or mark the file for management/legal review.

## Step 5 - Creditworthiness Decision

Prepare a written decision memo that states:

- Approved, declined, or approved with conditions.
- The verified income or asset base relied on.
- The full proposed monthly housing burden.
- Total monthly debts and recurring obligations considered.
- Debt-to-income ratio and/or residual income.
- Credit-history strengths and concerns.
- Compensating factors, if any.
- Required conditions before signing.
- Reviewer name and date.

Do not approve if the file only works by ignoring known debts, taxes, insurance, repairs, or a foreseeable balloon/refinance problem.

## Step 6 - Compliance Packet

Keep the following in the project or transaction folder:

- Transaction screen.
- Application package.
- Verification records.
- Payment and obligation worksheet.
- Ability-to-repay worksheet.
- Decision memo.
- Attorney review notes.
- Final signed buyer disclosures and sale documents.
- Evidence of any required notices, adverse-action letters, counseling certificates, or acknowledgements.

## Step 7 - Final Pre-Signing Review

Before signing:

- Confirm the seller-financer exclusion or loan-originator plan.
- Confirm no negative amortization.
- Confirm state-specific disclosure, recording, licensing, and forfeiture/foreclosure requirements.
- Confirm any required federal or state notices.
- Confirm the buyer received final terms with enough time for review.
- Confirm Wes or counsel has approved any exception.

## Open Design Questions

- What internal DTI or residual-income threshold should be used?
- Should Buy Your Home require a licensed mortgage loan originator for every consumer Contract for Deed?
- Should a balloon payment be prohibited as policy even where legally available?
- What minimum cash reserves should a tenant-buyer have after down payment and closing?
- What fair-credit and adverse-action notice workflow is required for declines?
