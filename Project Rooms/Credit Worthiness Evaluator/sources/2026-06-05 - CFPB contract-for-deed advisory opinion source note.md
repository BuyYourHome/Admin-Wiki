# CFPB Contract-for-Deed Advisory Opinion Source Note

## Source

- Title: Truth in Lending (Regulation Z); Consumer Protections for Home Sales Financed Under Contracts for Deed
- Publisher: Consumer Financial Protection Bureau
- URL: https://www.consumerfinance.gov/rules-policy/final-rules/truth-in-lending-regulation-z-consumer-protections-for-home-sales-financed-under-contracts-for-deed/
- Date issued: 2024-08-13
- Captured: 2026-06-05

## Status

Authoritative federal source for the CFPB's stated position that contracts for deed generally meet TILA / Regulation Z credit definitions when a creditor sells a home to a consumer using a contract for deed.

## Key Points For This Project

- CFPB says a consumer contract-for-deed home purchase will generally meet Regulation Z's definition of credit.
- When secured by the buyer's dwelling, the buyer will generally be entitled to residential mortgage-loan protections under TILA.
- The project should not treat the label "Contract for Deed" as a way around consumer-credit rules.

## Use In Drafting

Use this source to support the compliance premise that the tenant-buyer review process should document credit terms, borrower protections, and repayment ability rather than treating the transaction as a simple lease or ordinary purchase contract.
