# CFPB Regulation Z Section 1026.43 Source Note

## Source

- Title: 12 CFR 1026.43, Minimum standards for transactions secured by a dwelling
- Publisher: Consumer Financial Protection Bureau, Interactive Regulation Z
- URL: https://www.consumerfinance.gov/rules-policy/regulations/1026/43/
- Captured: 2026-06-05

## Status

Authoritative federal regulatory source for ability-to-repay factors and verification standards.

## Key Points For This Project

Section 1026.43 identifies the core ability-to-repay review factors:

- Current or reasonably expected income or assets, excluding the dwelling securing the loan.
- Current employment status if employment income is relied on.
- Monthly payment on the proposed transaction.
- Monthly payment on any simultaneous loan.
- Monthly mortgage-related obligations.
- Current debt obligations, alimony, and child support.
- Monthly debt-to-income ratio or residual income.
- Credit history.

The rule also emphasizes verification using reasonably reliable third-party records for information relied on in the repayment decision.

## Use In Drafting

Use these factors as the conservative underwriting framework for the tenant-buyer creditworthiness process, even if counsel later confirms that a particular one-property seller-financer exclusion reduces the formal ATR obligation.
