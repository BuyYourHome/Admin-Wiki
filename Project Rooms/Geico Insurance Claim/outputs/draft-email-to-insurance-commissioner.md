# Draft Email To Insurance Commissioner

To: North Carolina Department of Insurance / Commissioner Mike Causey

Subject: Request for assistance with GEICO property damage claim 0532806750000008

Commissioner Causey and Consumer Services Team,

I am requesting help with GEICO claim 0532806750000008, handled by Government Employees Insurance Company, arising from a collision/property damage loss involving my 2003 Cadillac Escalade.

GEICO has treated the property damage portion of the claim as a total loss. GEICO initially valued the vehicle at about $8,400. After I provided information that I had recently installed about $5,000 in upgrades, including an Alpine sound/navigation/camera system and other equipment, GEICO increased the valuation only to $8,631.98. The adjuster described the increase as including the premium radio and backup camera, but the added amount appears to be only about $232 and does not fairly account for the upgrades or replacement cost of what was taken from me.

I also submitted a lease document for the vehicle I was temporarily driving. GEICO said it needed a complete rental invoice and would review loss of use only until three days after the vehicle was declared a total loss. I would like GEICO to clearly explain its position on the loss-of-use portion of the claim as well.

On April 27, 2026, my attorney's office sent GEICO a Letter of Representation and Demand. On May 26, 2026, GEICO sent a "Total Loss Settlement Letter" for the claim. I forwarded that letter to my attorney and noted that the property adjuster's value was unchanged. I do not believe the property damage offer reflects a fair valuation of my loss, and I am asking for your office's help in getting GEICO to review and explain its valuation and claim-handling position.

The claim has also involved bodily injury communications, including a separate GEICO payment email for $3,030.72 described as a partial bodily injury payment. I am not asking your office to treat that bodily injury payment as a property damage settlement. My concern here is the property damage / total loss valuation, the treatment of the upgrades, and the loss-of-use issue.

On May 27, 2026, GEICO advised me that because I was represented by counsel for both property damage and bodily injury, I needed to direct questions through my attorney. I am willing to coordinate with my attorney and provide written permission if your office needs it before reviewing or communicating about this matter.

I understand that the Department may not be able to set the value of a claim or act as my legal representative. I am asking your office to use the assistance available to consumers, including forwarding the complaint to GEICO, requiring GEICO to explain its valuation and loss-of-use position, reviewing whether GEICO's handling complies with applicable North Carolina insurance requirements, and asking GEICO to reconsider the property damage offer in good faith based on the supporting documentation.

Please let me know what documents you need. I can provide the total loss settlement letter, GEICO claim communications, receipts for the upgrades, the rental/lease documentation, photos, valuation documents, and correspondence with the adjuster and counsel.

Thank you for your help.

Sincerely,
