# Planning Cost Framework

This cost analysis separates the project into three major cost categories:

1. Design and entitlement
2. Development and pre-construction
3. Build and delivery

The project is assumed to include:

* approximately **5,000-6,000 SF** of residential building area,
* 8 private residential suites,
* shared common areas,
* slab-on-grade construction,
* engineered onsite wastewater,
* private utility infrastructure,
* and long-term rental operation rather than speculative resale.

---

# 1. Design & Entitlement Costs

## Scope

Design and entitlement costs include the professional work required to move the project from concept through permit-ready status.

Typical included items:

* architectural concept design,
* schematic design and permit drawings,
* civil engineering,
* survey and site planning,
* soil and septic evaluation,
* wastewater engineering,
* Wake County coordination,
* Fuquay-Varina Urban Service Area coordination,
* legal/accounting structuring,
* permit applications,
* and entitlement-stage contingency.

## Planning Range

| Cost Item | Low | High |
|---|---:|---:|
| Architecture and building design | $35,000 | $90,000 |
| Civil engineering and site planning | $20,000 | $60,000 |
| Septic, soil, and wastewater engineering | $20,000 | $90,000 |
| Survey, testing, environmental, and due diligence | $10,000 | $30,000 |
| Permitting, review, and municipal/county coordination | $15,000 | $50,000 |
| Legal, accounting, and trust structuring | $15,000 | $40,000 |
| Design/entitlement contingency | $15,000 | $45,000 |
| **Estimated Design & Entitlement Total** | **$130,000** | **$405,000** |

## Notes

The highest-risk item in this category is the onsite wastewater path. If Wake County Environmental Services, the soil engineer, or wastewater engineer require a more advanced treatment system or reserve-field strategy, design and entitlement costs may move toward the high end of the range.

# 2. Development & Pre-Construction Costs

## Scope

Development costs include project costs incurred before and during construction that are not direct building costs.

Typical included items:

* sponsor/development management,
* owner representation,
* construction pricing and bidding,
* lender and financing costs,
* insurance,
* property carrying costs,
* accounting and administration,
* operating setup,
* furniture, fixtures, and equipment planning,
* lease-up preparation,
* and reserves.

# 3. Build & Delivery Costs

## Scope

Build costs include direct construction and delivery of the physical asset.

Typical included items:

* building construction,
* slab and foundation,
* framing,
* roofing,
* mechanical, electrical, and plumbing,
* private bathrooms and kitchenettes,
* shared kitchen and common areas,
* exterior improvements,
* parking and access,
* utility infrastructure,
* onsite wastewater system,
* construction contingency,
* furnishings,

# Preliminary Feasibility Observations

## Most Important Cost Drivers

The largest cost drivers are likely to be:

* building size and construction cost per square foot,
* number of private bathrooms and kitchenettes,
* engineered wastewater system requirements,
* sitework and utility infrastructure,
* financing terms,
* and contingency reserves.

## Relationship to Current Return Model

The workbook currently appears to model refinance, distributions, and investor returns using a **$650,000** debt/refinance assumption and the stated Class A/Class B/sponsor basis assumptions. If actual total project cost is materially above the modeled capital stack, the workbook should be updated before investor-facing use.
