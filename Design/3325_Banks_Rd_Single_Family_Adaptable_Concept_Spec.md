# 3325 Banks Rd Single-Family Adaptable Concept Spec

## Purpose

This is the third project direction for 3325 Banks Rd.

The concept shifts the project away from an investor-facing co-living plan and toward a single-family residence that may be more acceptable to partners, lenders, neighbors, and future buyers.

The design should still preserve infrastructure logic that could support a later build-out into something similar to the prior co-living / suite-based concept if that ever becomes desirable.

Core idea:

**Build a normal single-family residence first. Design it so the plumbing, bedroom stack, and circulation do not prevent later adaptation.**

## Format Rule

Use SVG or another controlled drawing format when geometry matters.

AI-generated PNG images may be useful for inspiration, but they have repeatedly made layout mistakes such as:

- bedrooms extending outside the post-frame wall line,
- open-to-below areas placed over bedrooms,
- doors opening into stairs or level changes,
- bedrooms without logical bathroom access,
- inconsistent stacked-floor geometry.

For floor plan logic, SVG is preferred because walls, rooms, labels, and circulation can be revised exactly.

## Building Shell

The concept should read as post-frame construction.

Constraints:

- Use a simple rectangular exterior building shell.
- Keep exterior walls straight.
- Do not allow bedrooms or other rooms to project past the main exterior wall line unless a porch or intentional appendage is specifically requested.
- Maintain the visual logic of a simple post-frame footprint.
- Avoid complicated jogs, bump-outs, angled walls, or irregular exterior geometry.

Current assumption:

- Conceptual two-story rectangular shell.
- Approximate 6,000 SF total building area remains the working project scale.
- Floor plan is conceptual only and is not a construction drawing, code review, engineering plan, or permit plan.

## Main Program Direction

The residence should function as a single-family home first.

Primary single-family components:

- 4 bedrooms total.
- Each bedroom should have its own bath.
- Open kitchen / dining / great room.
- Laundry.
- Mudroom.
- Pantry.
- Powder room.
- Two baths downstairs, one for each downstairs bedroom.
- Two baths upstairs, one for each upstairs bedroom.
- Covered porch.
- Covered outdoor living.
- Loft / overlook circulation upstairs.

Adaptability components:

- Stacked wet-wall plumbing logic.
- Future plumbing chase only where compact and necessary.
- Future suite conversion zone.
- Bedroom stack that could later support suite-style conversion.
- Open living volume that improves single-family appeal.

## Bedroom Placement

Current direction:

- All bedrooms should be on one side of the building.
- Two bedrooms should be on the first floor.
- Two bedrooms should be directly above them on the second floor.
- Bedrooms should stack cleanly floor-to-floor.
- Bedrooms should remain inside the straight rectangular exterior wall line.

First floor:

- Bedroom 1 and Bedroom 2 are on the bedroom side of the house.
- Each downstairs bedroom should have its own bath.
- They should connect to the main circulation, not dead-end or isolated spaces.

Second floor:

- Bedroom 3 and Bedroom 4 are directly above Bedroom 1 and Bedroom 2.
- Both upstairs bedrooms must connect to same-level upstairs hall / loft circulation.
- Each upstairs bedroom should have its own bath.
- Bedroom 4 must not open directly into a stair shaft, stair landing at another level, or isolated dead-end.
- Bedroom 4 must have bathroom access from the same-level hall / loft circulation.
- Bedroom 4 should have its own bath.
- The stair landing must not occupy or block the upstairs hall serving Bedroom 4.
- Bedroom 4 must have a clear same-level path to the upstairs bath and loft/hall without passing through a stair run.
- Bedroom 3 must also have logical bathroom access.
- The stair should read as one continuous stair: up from the first floor and down/arrival on the second floor. Do not draw it as going up in both directions.

## Open-To-Below Constraint

The open-to-below space is allowed only over the open living area.

Required:

- Open-to-below should be over the great room / dining / kitchen side.
- Open-to-below must not be over Bedroom 1 or Bedroom 2.
- Open-to-below must not be over any bedroom wing.
- Railings should be shown around the open-to-below area.
- The open volume should reinforce single-family appeal, not create privacy problems above bedrooms.

## Circulation Rules

The plan must be legible as a real house.

Required:

- Every bedroom must have a clear path to the hall / loft / main circulation.
- Every bedroom must have bathroom access.
- Each bedroom should have its own bath unless a later instruction changes this.
- A door should not open to a stairway unless it is a proper same-level landing or hall.
- A stair landing cannot substitute for the bedroom hall if it blocks bedroom-to-bath circulation.
- The upstairs stair arrival should connect to a loft hall.
- The loft hall should connect Bedroom 3, Bedroom 4, the upstairs bath, and any overlook / loft lounge.
- First-floor circulation should connect bedrooms, bath, open living space, kitchen, mudroom, and exterior entries.

## Plumbing Logic

The design should preserve useful plumbing structure from the prior suite/co-living concept without wasting usable floor area.

Required:

- Keep baths, kitchen, laundry, powder, and any future plumbing chase logically grouped where possible.
- Stack upstairs baths over or near first-floor baths.
- Use wet walls and compact plumbing alignment, not a large dedicated plumbing room.
- Give usable floor area to the bathrooms and bedrooms instead of reserving an oversized plumbing core/chase.
- Avoid scattering plumbing fixtures across the entire footprint without reason.

Purpose:

- Reduce future construction complexity.
- Preserve option value for later suite conversion.
- Keep the single-family residence practical and efficient.

## Future Conversion Logic

This version should not present as a co-living project first.

It should present as:

**single-family residence first, adaptable later.**

Future conversion elements should be subtle and secondary.

Possible future conversion zones:

- Bedroom wing.
- Stacked wet walls.
- Upstairs baths aligned with downstairs baths.
- Hall / loft area that could support future suite access.

Avoid:

- Making the plan look like a rooming house.
- Over-labeling future co-living functions.
- Making the single-family plan feel compromised by the future use.
